# Source Notes

The lab is inspired by the research-design logic in Meyer and Mok on disability dynamics and Albanesi and Kim on heterogeneous labor-market incidence. It does not use their confidential, administrative, survey, or private-sector data.

The synthetic data encode transparent mechanisms:

- disability onset reduces work capacity after event time 0;
- firm accommodation partially offsets capacity loss for some workers;
- benefit receipt changes insurance and labor-supply incentives;
- household buffers partly protect consumption;
- job mobility and employment exit generate dynamic selection;
- welfare differs from earnings because capacity, flexibility, insurance, mobility, and accommodation matter.
