### Monopsony, accommodation dependence, and job lock

| Channel | Why labor supply becomes more inelastic | Labor-market implication |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Employer-provided insurance | Leaving the job may mean losing coverage or provider networks | Employer can retain workers at lower effective outside options [@madrian1994] |
| Accommodation dependence | Worker may need a customized schedule/task setup that is uncertain elsewhere | Current employer has quasi-rents from accommodation-specific match |
| Local scarcity of feasible jobs | Disability/chronic conditions shrink the set of acceptable jobs and commutes | Lower quit elasticity and higher monopsony power |
| Stigma and uncertainty | Workers face risk that new employers will not accommodate or will discriminate | Search costs rise and wage competition weakens [@ameriSchurAdyaBentleyEtAl2018] |
| Program interaction | Fear of losing benefits or facing reassessment can discourage mobility | Participation and job transitions respond sluggishly |
