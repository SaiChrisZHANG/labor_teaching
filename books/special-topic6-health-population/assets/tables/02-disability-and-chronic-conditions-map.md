## Disability and chronic conditions: core labor margins

| Object | Main labor margin | Typical outcome | Why it matters for labor economics |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Acute disability onset | Short-run hours / participation | Absence, reduced hours, temporary earnings loss | Identifies immediate work-capacity effects |
| Chronic conditions | Long-run attachment and occupation | Persistent wage loss, occupational downgrading, exit | Captures career reallocation and scarring |
| Functional limitations | Feasible job set | Narrower occupation / schedule set | Changes reservation wages and matching |
| Disability-program status | Employment boundary and program interaction | Benefit receipt, nonemployment, dynamic exit | Mixes health and institutional incentives |
| Accommodation access | Within-job retention | Retention, task redesign, job quality | Shows employer-side mediation |
| Remote / flexible work access | Search and job choice | Expanded feasible set, better attachment | Reveals importance of commuting and condition control |
