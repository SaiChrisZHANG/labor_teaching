## Adjustment frictions, lock-in, and inequality

| Friction / channel | Labor consequence | Why it can create inequality or monopsony power |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Employer-provided health insurance | Job lock / reduced mobility | Workers stay in suboptimal jobs to retain coverage [@madrian1994] |
| Employer-specific accommodations | Accommodation lock | Outside options become less credible if other firms will not accommodate |
| Commuting or schedule constraints | Narrower feasible job set | Health-limited workers face fewer acceptable employers/jobs |
| Information frictions about accommodations and rights | Lower search and take-up | Better-informed or higher-resource workers capture more opportunities |
| Benefit interaction / waiting periods | Delayed or distorted adjustment | Workers may exit prematurely or stay too long in poor matches |
| Remote-work access | Constraint relief | Differential access to remote-friendly jobs can widen inequality |
| Treatment affordability / continuity | Unequal return to work | Access to treatment changes the ability to remain attached |
