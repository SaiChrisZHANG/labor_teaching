## Measurement and identification problems

| Measurement choice | What it captures | What it may miss | Why it matters for labor estimates |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-reported health | Private information on functioning and pain | Reporting heterogeneity, justification bias | May correlate strongly with work-relevant latent health |
| Diagnosed conditions | Medical recognition of illness | Undiagnosed conditions, severity, functional limits | Can look objective but still be incomplete |
| Disability status | Health + administrative classification + incentives | Underlying untreated health, borderline cases | Blends health with program rules |
| Hospitalization / acute shock | Severe discrete events | Everyday chronic limitations and gradual decline | Useful for causal designs but narrow in scope |
| Mortality risk | Severe underlying health risk | Nonfatal functional constraints | Strong for severity, weak for routine work capacity |
| Mental health scales | Stress, depression, cognition, functioning | Stigma, underreporting, treatment effects | Central but especially noisy and endogenous |
