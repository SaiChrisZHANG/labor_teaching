### Short-run versus long-run effects and treatment/accommodation

| Object | Short-run effect | Long-run effect | Why the distinction matters |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Acute hospitalization | Immediate nonemployment, earnings drop, liquidity shock | Partial recovery or persistent scarring depending on severity and insurance | Identifies shock incidence but not chronic adaptation on its own [@dobkinFinkelsteinKluenderNotowidigdo2018hospital] |
| Disability onset | Initially limited change if workers stay attached | Larger persistent effects through exit, reduced hours, and occupational change | Event-study timing clarifies when costs materialize [@collischonHiesingerPohlan2023; @meyerMok2019] |
| Chronic conditions | May look small in simple cross sections | Often large cumulative losses through repeated absences, fatigue, and reduced career mobility | Static estimates can understate true incidence [@meyerMok2019] |
| Accommodation / treatment | Can stabilize work in the short run | Can delay exit or lower SSDI claiming over longer horizons | Shows that labor-market effects are not technologically fixed [@hillMaestasMullen2016; @zaresani2018] |
