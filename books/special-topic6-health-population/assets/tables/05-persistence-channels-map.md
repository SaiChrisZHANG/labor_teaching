## Persistence channels and adjustment margins

| Shock type | Worker-side margin | Firm-side margin | Local/aggregate adjustment | Key labor object |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Infectious disease epidemic | attendance, participation, mortality risk, caregiving | scheduling, safety protocols, technology substitution, hiring | wages, sectoral collapse/recovery, migration, closures | employment, wages, participation, welfare |
| Environmental exposure (pollution, heat) | productivity, fatigue, hours, absenteeism, health deterioration | shift timing, task redesign, investment, location choices | compensating differentials, local labor supply, spatial sorting | productivity, job quality, real wages |
| Mortality shock / severe health crisis | labor scarcity, family labor reallocation, savings behavior | recruitment, capital substitution, reorganization | bargaining power, institutional response, migration | wages, skill premia, inequality |
| Aging / demographic change | retirement, late-career work, caregiving, labor-force participation | automation, recruitment, accommodation, age-specific job design | labor shortages, migration, automation adoption | labor supply, wage structure, productivity |
| Fertility decline / dependency change | lifecycle labor supply, savings, care burden | benefit design, scheduling, hiring of older workers | demographic composition, fiscal pressure, migration | hours, retirement, intergenerational incidence |

Interpretation: the same health or demographic shock can change labor markets through multiple margins at once. A good paper makes clear whether the relevant response is worker capacity, firm adaptation, migration/reallocation, or institutional/policy change.
