### Frontier methods box: what strong causal designs look like here

| Design family | What it buys you | Representative papers | Main cautions |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Acute-shock event study | Sharp timing around unexpected health deterioration | [@dobkinFinkelsteinKluenderNotowidigdo2018hospital; @garciaGomezJonesRice2013] | Shock severity and treatment response both matter |
| Disability-onset admin event study | Long-run labor-market dynamics after recognized disability status | [@collischonHiesingerPohlan2023] | Disability status is partly institutional, not purely medical |
| Program-rule / threshold designs | Separates benefit incentives from underlying health | [@frenchSong2014; @zaresani2018] | Local average treatment effect may be narrow |
| Employer accommodation designs | Identifies firm-side margins and retention effects | [@hillMaestasMullen2016] | Accommodation is endogenous to worker and firm traits |
| Twin / sibling comparisons | Reduces family-background and endowment confounding | Within-family health/labor applications; related designs discussed in [@bockermanCawley2018] | External validity and within-family measurement remain concerns |
| Genetic / Mendelian-randomization designs | Uses genetic predisposition as an instrument for chronic conditions or risk factors | [@bockermanCawley2018; @vonHinkeKesslerScholder2016] | Exclusion restrictions and population structure require caution |
| Matched employer–employee heterogeneity | Shows whether some firms buffer health shocks better than others | [@hillMaestasMullen2016; @biroGerardGyetvaiPapp2024] | Firm sorting can confound simple comparisons |
