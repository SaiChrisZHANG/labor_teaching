## Methods and design map

| Empirical setting | Common design | Typical econometric methods | What a good design must show |
|---|---|---|---|
| Local demand shocks across labor markets | Shift-share / Bartik exposure | IV, panel FE, event studies | Why exposure is exogenous and which margin adjusts |
| Migration incidence on receiving places | Predicted-destination IV | 2SLS, distributed lags | Why migrant allocation is not endogenous to local trends |
| Commuting-cost changes | Infrastructure / travel-time shocks | Event study, IV, structural gravity | Distinguish commuting from demand confounds |
| Persistence after recessions | Dynamic place-event design | Event study, local projections | Separate place recovery from incumbent-worker outcomes |
| Gentrification and neighborhood change | Shift-share or job-location shocks | Panel FE, instrumented tract exposure | Show labor-market mechanism, not just price changes |
| Diversification / resilience | Shock heterogeneity by local industrial mix | Interaction designs, reduced form + structural welfare | Clarify whether diversity changes reallocation or insurance |
| Spatial-equilibrium incidence | Calibrated / sufficient-statistics framework | GMM, simulated equilibrium, welfare decomposition | Translate wages into real opportunity and welfare |
