:::{table} Where the urban labor field is and where it is going
:label: tbl:urban-field-trajectory

| Area | What is well established | Where the frontier is moving |
|---|---|---|
| Spatial equilibrium | Wages, rents, and amenities jointly determine real labor-market opportunity. | Dynamic incidence under remote work, climate change, and zoning reform. |
| Housing and labor access | Housing supply constraints can block access to high-productivity places. | Distributional effects of housing reform and who captures gains. |
| Segregation and neighborhood exposure | Neighborhoods affect access, search, and long-run opportunity. | Mechanism separation: schools, networks, safety, stigma, and local institutions. |
| Risk and the feasible set | Crime, pollution, and heat affect work feasibility and welfare. | Hidden harms, real-time exposure data, and compensating differentials under unequal information. |
| Local shocks and reallocation | Migration and commuting shape adjustment to local demand changes. | Slow adjustment, persistence, place-based policy, and mobility barriers by wealth/skill/family status. |
| Urban labor power | Space interacts with firm choice and outside options. | Commuting-linked monopsony, remote work, and local labor market power. |

:::
