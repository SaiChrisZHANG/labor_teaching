:::{table} Portability, relevance, and critique in urban labor research
:label: tbl:urban-portability-relevance

| Challenge | Weak version | Stronger version |
|---|---|---|
| Setting-specificity | “This city is interesting.” | “This setting reveals a general mechanism linking spatial frictions to labor outcomes.” |
| Mechanism ambiguity | “Neighborhoods matter.” | “Transit access, school quality, rents, and networks each imply different counterfactuals.” |
| Ignoring equilibrium | “Treated workers gain.” | “Workers, firms, landlords, commuters, and migrants all adjust; incidence must be traced.” |
| Nominal-outcome bias | “Wages increased.” | “Real access, commute burden, risk, and child opportunity may move differently from wages.” |
| Geography mismatch | “County-level effects show neighborhood mechanisms.” | “The unit of analysis matches the mechanism under study.” |
| Limited transportability | “This policy worked here.” | “Here is why the mechanism should or should not generalize to other housing, transit, or institutional regimes.” |

:::
