# Methods and design map

| Design family | Best use | Typical shortfall |
|---|---|---|
| Spatial equilibrium / quantitative counterfactual | Aggregate misallocation, welfare incidence, equilibrium adjustment | Easy to lose sight of which micro friction is actually binding |
| Bartik / shift-share local labor-demand shocks | Wage/rent/employment incidence after shocks | Often understate commuting and overstate migration as the only margin |
| Housing-supply elasticity heterogeneity | Test whether adjustment occurs through prices instead of population | Elasticity measures can be noisy and slow-moving |
| Migration response / event-study designs | Timing and heterogeneity of worker relocation | Need to separate moving-cost frictions from preferences or insurance |
| Transport / commuting shocks | Access without relocation | May capture broader urban change, not only labor access |
| Reduced-form wage premium comparisons | Nominal urban premium and rent-inclusive premium | Easy to mistake nominal wage gains for welfare gains |
| Administrative linkage designs | Rich worker/firm/place heterogeneity | Data access and measurement of actual residence can be restrictive |
