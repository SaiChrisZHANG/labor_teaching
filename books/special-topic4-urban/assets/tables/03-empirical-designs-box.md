# Empirical designs box

| Design family | Identifying variation | Best for | Main threat | Representative papers |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mover / age-at-move design | Differential exposure timing when families move | Long-run neighborhood effects on labor outcomes | Endogenous moves and disruption | [@chettyHendrenKatz2016MTO; @chettyHendren2018] |
| Cohort or cumulative exposure design | Variation in years exposed to neighborhood conditions | Dynamic exposure and developmental timing | Omitted family trends | [@chettyHendren2018] |
| Policy shocks | MTO vouchers, public housing demolition, boundary changes, transit or job-location shocks | Access or neighborhood change caused by policy | Multiple channels activated at once | [@chettyHendrenKatz2016MTO; @chynPublicHousingDemolition2018; @millerWhenWorkMoves2023] |
| School attendance / admission design | Boundaries, lotteries, assignment discontinuities | Separating neighborhood from school effects | Capitalization into housing and selection around boundaries | [@blackDoBetterSchools1999; @monarrezSchoolAttendance2021] |
| Audit / correspondence design | Randomized address, distance, or neighborhood reputation in applications | Employer beliefs, stigma, and hiring discrimination | External validity to realized labor-market equilibrium | [@phillipsDoLowWageEmployers2020] |
| Matched employer-employee / network design | Neighbor-coworker overlap, establishment location, workplace microdata | Referral networks and spatial job access | Reflection problems and sorting into neighborhoods/firms | [@bayerRossTopa2008InformalHiringNetworks; @hellersteinMcInerneyNeumark2011; @hellersteinKutzbachNeumark2014] |
