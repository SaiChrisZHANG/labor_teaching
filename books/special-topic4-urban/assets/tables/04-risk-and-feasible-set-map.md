:::{table} Risk, safety, and the urban feasible set for work
:label: tbl:risk-feasible-set-map

| Margin | Labor-market object | Typical mechanism | Example outcomes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Public safety / crime | Search radius, commute timing, neighborhood choice | Higher travel risk or fear reduces feasible job set | Employment, accepted wage, commute distance |
| Workplace harassment / violence | Retention, sector choice, within-firm mobility | Unsafe work conditions raise disutility of certain jobs | Turnover, segregation, wage inequality |
| Pollution / heat / noise | Productivity, hours, absenteeism, schedule choice | Exposure worsens health or cognitive performance | Output, attendance, hours, fatigue |
| Environmental amenities | Job and residence sorting | Workers trade wages against exposure/amenity bundles | Compensating differentials, real welfare |
| Hidden harms | Welfare vs observed wages | Risk not fully priced or not fully observed | Wages become incomplete welfare measures |

:::
