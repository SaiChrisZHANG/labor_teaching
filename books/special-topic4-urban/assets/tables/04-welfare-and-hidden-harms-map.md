:::{table} Welfare and hidden harms checklist
:label: tbl:welfare-hidden-harms-map

| Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Does the measured outcome capture welfare, or just nominal earnings? | Risk and exposure may reduce welfare without lowering wages proportionally. |
| Are workers selecting into exposure? | Endogenous sorting can make dangerous jobs or neighborhoods look benign. |
| Are there unmeasured schedule or timing margins? | Workers may avoid late shifts or specific commute windows even if employment is unchanged. |
| Does the design capture firm and landlord responses? | Risk can be capitalized into rents, staffing patterns, or workplace technology. |
| Is the comparison group exposed to a different hidden harm? | Many “clean” counterfactuals still differ in fear, stress, or commute burden. |

:::
