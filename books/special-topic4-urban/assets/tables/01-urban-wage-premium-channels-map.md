| Channel | Mechanism | Labor interpretation | Example paper |
|---|---|---|---|
| Productivity / agglomeration | Dense places raise firm or worker productivity | Higher wages may reflect true productivity gains | [@moretti2011] |
| Learning | Cities speed human-capital accumulation | Wage premium grows over the career rather than appearing instantly | [@glaeserMare2001] |
| Sorting | More productive workers choose dense places | Observed premium may partly reflect worker composition | [@glaeserMare2001] |
| Compensating differential | Workers require higher pay for rents, congestion, or other costs | High wages need not imply high welfare | [@roback1982]; [@albouyLue2015] |
| Spatial access / work geography | Job location changes who can feasibly work there | Wage and employment patterns depend on job access, not just skill prices | [@millerWhenWorkMoves2023] |
