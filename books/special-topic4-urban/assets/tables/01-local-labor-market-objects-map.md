| Object | Labor meaning | Why it matters in Week 1 |
|---|---|---|
| Workplace wage | Earnings at the job location | Must be separated from residential cost of living |
| Residential rent / housing cost | Cost of accessing a place in which work occurs | Changes the real value of nominal pay |
| Commuting cost | Time, money, and effort needed to connect residence and job | Shrinks the effective opportunity set |
| Amenities / disamenities | Safety, schools, services, congestion, environment, social life | Change welfare and compensating differentials |
| Job access | Reachable jobs within a generalized travel-cost budget | Determines the feasible labor-market set |
| Local labor market | Search and competition space created by commuting and mobility frictions | More meaningful than a simple administrative boundary |
| Urban wage premium | Higher wages in denser places | May reflect productivity, learning, sorting, or compensation |
| Outside option | Best attainable alternative in nearby or connected places | Changes search, bargaining, and welfare interpretation |
