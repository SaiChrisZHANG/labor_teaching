:::{table} Frontier project opportunities in urban labor
:label: tbl:urban-frontier-project-opportunities

| Frontier topic | Sample research question | Useful data / design |
|---|---|---|
| Zoning reform and labor access | Do housing reforms improve access to high-wage labor markets for constrained workers? | Parcel/zoning data, rents, LEHD, ACS, event study / boundary design |
| Remote work and spatial opportunity | Does remote work relax geographic barriers or widen spatial inequality? | Job postings, remote-work measures, worker panels, difference-in-differences |
| Commuting and labor market power | Do commuting costs raise employer wage-setting power? | Matched worker-firm data, commute shocks, structural or reduced-form designs |
| Climate and urban labor | How do heat and pollution change labor supply, productivity, and sorting across cities? | Weather, pollution monitors, worker/firm productivity data |
| Redevelopment and displacement | Do redevelopment projects improve labor outcomes for incumbents or mainly re-sort residents? | Parcel/neighborhood panels, tract employment, migration/address histories |
| Child opportunity in cities | Which neighborhood changes translate into long-run labor-market gains for children? | Administrative child outcome data, housing mobility or redevelopment designs |

:::
