# Data and measurement map

| Data source | What it measures well | Typical use in this topic | Important limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| LEHD / LODES / QWI | Residence-workplace links, flows, establishment geography | Job access, workplace location, suburbanization, local labor demand | Limited demographics, access restrictions for some uses |
| Decennial Census / ACS / PUMS | Residence, commuting, earnings, demographics | Segregation, spatial mismatch, commuting, neighborhood sorting | Weaker establishment detail than matched employer-employee data |
| Opportunity Atlas / tax-linked data | Childhood neighborhoods and adult outcomes | Mover designs, exposure timing, long-run labor opportunity | Limited direct workplace mechanisms |
| School assignment / boundary data | School access and local institutions | Separating school from neighborhood channels | Boundaries may capitalize into housing and sorting |
| Transit / GTFS / travel-time matrices | Generalized access to jobs | Transit shocks, travel-time inequality, access maps | Requires careful geography and mode assumptions |
| Audit / correspondence data | Employer response to addresses or commute distance | Neighborhood stigma, hiring discrimination | Usually initial callback margin only |
| Matched employer-employee data | Worker-firm matches and coworker location | Network and referral designs, workplace segregation | Often confidential and institution-specific |
| Housing-program or relocation admin data | Exogenous or policy-induced moves | Neighborhood choice barriers, public housing demolition, vouchers | Treatment bundles may activate many channels |
