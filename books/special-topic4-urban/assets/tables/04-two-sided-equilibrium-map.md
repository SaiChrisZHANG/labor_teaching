:::{table} Two-sided equilibrium map: labor opportunities and urban risk
:label: tbl:two-sided-equilibrium-map

| Direction | Question | Typical empirical object | Interpretation challenge |
|---|---|---|---|
| Risk -> labor | How does safety/environment alter work? | Participation, hours, search radius, productivity, quits | Exposure is endogenous to residence and job choice |
| Labor -> crime | How do weak labor opportunities affect crime? | Job loss, local labor demand, earnings prospects, arrests | Crime responds through multiple channels, not only “labor supply to crime” |
| Risk -> sorting | Who remains in risky places/jobs? | Worker composition, migration, firm turnover | Selection can mimic treatment effects |
| Risk -> rents/wages | Who captures compensating differentials? | Wages, rents, commuting, firm productivity | Incidence may fall on workers, landlords, firms, or entrants |
| Joint equilibrium | Which wedges survive once firms and workers adjust? | Changes in crime, rents, wages, migration, exposure | Reduced-form estimates may already embed equilibrium adjustment |

:::
