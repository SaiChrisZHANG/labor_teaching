:::{table} Data and empirical methods commonly used in this literature
:label: tbl:data-methods-map

| Topic | Common data | Typical designs | What a good design must clarify |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crime and labor opportunities | Arrest records, layoffs, matched employer-employee data, local labor markets | Layoff shocks, industry shocks, local labor demand shifts, event studies | Whether the mechanism is earnings, unemployment, credit, or informal substitution |
| Pollution and labor supply | Pollution monitors/satellites, worker panels, time use, admin labor records | Plant closures, weather inversions, high-frequency variation, panel fixed effects | Whether the outcome is supply, health, or productivity |
| Workplace safety / harassment | Surveys, administrative HR data, complaints, linked payroll panels | Within-firm heterogeneity, audit/reporting variation, event studies | Whether reporting incentives contaminate measured exposure |
| Heat / noise / productivity | Fine weather/noise data, piece-rate or task-level output | High-frequency panel designs, within-worker exposure changes | Whether adaptation or selection attenuates estimates |
| Urban safety and commuting | Travel-time matrices, neighborhood crime data, mobile or transit data | Policy shocks, route changes, transit changes, spatial discontinuities | Whether changes operate through mobility, fear, timing, or actual victimization |

:::
