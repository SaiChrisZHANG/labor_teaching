# Mechanisms map

| Mechanism | Main labor-market object | What it changes | Typical outcomes | Different from |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Distance to jobs / spatial mismatch | Reachable vacancies | Search set, interview probability, commuting feasibility | Employment, unemployment duration, commute, reservation job set | Networks, stigma, peers |
| Transit and commuting access | Generalized travel cost | Effective access to jobs and schedules | Employment, job quality, commute burden, labor-force attachment | Migration, neighborhood exposure |
| Employer stigma / address discrimination | Callback and hiring | Whether employers treat residence as information or signal | Callbacks, hiring, job quality | Pure physical access |
| Residential labor-market networks | Informal referrals and coworker links | Information, trust, job finding through neighbors | Hiring, job quality, establishment match, recovery after shocks | Pure peer effects |
| Neighborhood peers / social capital | Norms, aspirations, information | Human-capital investment, search behavior, expectations | Earnings, employment, mobility, schooling | Employer-side discrimination |
| Schools / public goods / local institutions | Skill formation and local opportunity | Long-run job access and human capital | Adult earnings, employment, mobility | Immediate commute access |
| Safety / local conditions | Feasible set and stress | Search radius, work hours, willingness to travel | Participation, retention, welfare | Wage-only channels |
