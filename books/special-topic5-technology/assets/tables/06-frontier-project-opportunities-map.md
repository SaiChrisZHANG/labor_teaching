## Frontier Project Opportunities Map

| Frontier direction | Labor question | Why it is promising |
|---|---|---|
| Generative AI use at work | Who gets augmented, who is displaced, and how quickly do performance gains diffuse? | Worker-use data and platform data are creating new direct measures |
| Firm adoption and reorganization | How does AI change vacancies, supervision, delegation, promotion, and training? | Adoption is observable earlier than long-run equilibrium outcomes |
| Technology and labor-market power | Does technology increase employer rents, algorithmic control, or worker surveillance? | Connects technology research to labor-share and monopsony debates |
| Global service trade and outsourcing | Which tasks become tradable and which workers/places gain or lose? | AI may reopen core questions about trade in services and urban geography |
| Infrastructure and labor | How do compute, energy, and data-center investments reshape local labor demand and land use? | Fast-moving, policy-relevant, and still lightly studied by labor economists |
| Measurement projects | What is the right measure of technology for a given labor question? | Measurement itself is one of the highest-return margins in the field |
