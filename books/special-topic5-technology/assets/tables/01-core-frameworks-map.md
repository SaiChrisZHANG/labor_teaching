:::{table} Core frameworks for technology and labor
:label: tbl:tech-core-frameworks
| Framework | Main object | Main labor question | Typical outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| Skill-biased technical change | Relative productivity of skills | Which skills become more valuable? | Skill premia, wage inequality |
| Task-based / routine-biased change | Allocation of tasks across labor and capital | Which tasks are automated or complemented? | Employment by occupation/task |
| Automation / new-task framework | Displacement versus reinstatement | Does technology shrink labor’s task set or expand it elsewhere? | Labor demand, labor share, employment |
| AI / prediction framework | Automation of prediction and information processing | Which decisions, workflows, or cognitive tasks change? | Hiring, supervision, decision support, demand shifts |
| Firm-adoption framework | Technology as firm-level organizational choice | Who adopts, how do they reorganize, and what happens to workers? | Employment mix, training, productivity, reallocation |
:::
