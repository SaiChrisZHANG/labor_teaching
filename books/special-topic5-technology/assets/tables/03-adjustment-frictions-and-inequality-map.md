### Adjustment frictions and inequality

| Friction | How it slows adjustment | Why it may widen inequality | Illustrative papers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Information frictions | workers do not know which skills are newly valuable | better-connected workers adapt earlier | [@demingNoray2020] |
| Inertia / present bias / limited memory | workers delay switching or adoption of new tools/training | delays are more costly for vulnerable workers | [@gertlerJohnsonVillarreal2025] |
| Distrust of new technologies or providers | workers avoid new platforms, tools, or training opportunities | distrust can be stratified by prior experience and resources | [@gertlerJohnsonVillarreal2025] |
| Financial constraints | training takes time and money | low-wealth workers cannot finance adjustment | common concern across training literature |
| Skill specificity | existing human capital does not transfer easily | workers in highly specific fields bear larger losses | [@smeets2025] |
| Employer training choices | firms retrain some workers but not others | incumbent/new-hire training splits can raise inequality | [@muehlemann2024] |
