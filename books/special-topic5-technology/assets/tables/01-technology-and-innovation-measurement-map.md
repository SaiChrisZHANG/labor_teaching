:::{table} Measurement of technology and innovation in labor research
:label: tbl:tech-measurement-map
| Proxy / measure | Closest object | Strength | Main limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Patent counts | Invention | Good for frontier innovation and timing | Weak on actual adoption |
| Patent text / task similarity | Capability content of technology | Connects innovation to tasks | Still distant from realized use |
| Robot installations | Embodied automation adoption | Concrete and interpretable | Narrow technology class |
| Software / IT adoption | Organizational and digital change | Closer to workplace transformation | Often heterogeneous and hard to compare |
| Job-posting text | Employer demand for tech-related skills | High-frequency and task-rich | Reflects desired demand, not realized use |
| Task surveys / O*NET | Job content and exposure structure | Transparent mapping to occupations | Can lag actual workplace change |
| Firm adoption / investment / resumes | Realized firm-level adoption | Closer to implementation and reorganization | Data access and comparability are difficult |
:::
