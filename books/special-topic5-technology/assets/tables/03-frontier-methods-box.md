### Frontier methods box: identifying worker adjustment under technological change

| Design family | What it identifies well | Main limitation | Representative papers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Admissions / major / track cutoff RD | causal returns to field choice or technology-relevant training | local to cutoff; education choice is only one adjustment margin | [@kirkeboenLeuvenMogstad2021]; [@silliman2022]; [@bleemerMehta2022] |
| Matched employer–employee event studies | within-firm adaptation around adoption events | adoption timing may still reflect selection unless designed carefully | [@genz2021] |
| Worker-level technology exposure measures | differential worker incidence of technological change | exposure construction is often contestable | [@koganPapanikolaouSchmidtSeegmiller2021] |
| Local exposure designs linked to training / retirement | how local shocks affect adjustment margins in equilibrium | mixes worker and place responses unless carefully interpreted | [@bertermann2025] |
| Firm-level AI adoption panels | incumbent vs new-hire training, task reorganization | short panels and self-reported adoption can be noisy | [@muehlemann2024]; [@babinaFedykHeHodson2024] |
| Task-content difference-in-differences | changes in task bundles under digital adoption | task measurement often depends on survey or classification quality | [@almeidaDixCarneiro2025] |

**What strong designs in this area usually do**
- define the adjustment margin precisely,
- connect exposure to a clear technological mechanism,
- separate treatment from sorting,
- observe dynamic outcomes,
- and measure incidence at the worker level whenever possible.
