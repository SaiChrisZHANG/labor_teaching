## Historical comparison map

| Episode | Main technology | Main labor-demand lesson | Why it matters for AI today | Representative papers |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Steam/mechanization | machine power and routinized production | displacement and productivity can coexist | clarifies that automation anxiety is not new | [@atackBatemanMargo2020inanimatePower]; [@mokyrVickersZiebarth2015historyAnxiety] |
| Electrification | general-purpose power technology with reorganization | large gains often required complementary organizational change | useful analogy for AI adoption with workflow redesign | [@fiszbeinLafortuneVamplew2020electrification] |
| Computerization / ICT | digital substitution in routine cognitive tasks | polarization and skill complementarity | direct precursor to modern task-based AI work | [@autorLevyMurnane2003SkillContent]; [@goldinKatz1998origins] |
| Robotics | embodied automation in production tasks | local displacement, heterogeneous firm effects | benchmark modern automation evidence | [@acemogluRestrepo2020Robots]; [@acemogluLelargeRestrepo2020competingRobots] |
| Generative AI | cognitive assistance / partial automation | rapid within-job productivity changes, uncertain equilibrium effects | current frontier with novel measurement and diffusion | [@brynjolfssonLiRaymond2025GenerativeAI]; [@aghionBergeaudBoppartKlenowLi2025differentUsesAI]; [@huiReshefZhou2025shortTermGenAI] |
