### AI attitudes, trust, and principal-agent tensions

| Margin | Worker concern | Manager concern | Labor implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Replacement risk | job loss / de-skilling | cost reduction vs morale | adoption may lower trust and raise resistance |
| Fairness / opacity | black-box evaluation, biased decisions | liability / explainability | attitudes shape acceptance of screening and evaluation tools |
| Monitoring / privacy | surveillance, autonomy loss | control, effort, risk | technology may raise effort but worsen job quality |
| Delegation | loss of discretion | loss of control or accountability | firms choose whether AI complements or substitutes managerial authority |
| Training burden | unequal access to augmentation | implementation cost | firms may widen inequality if only some workers are upskilled |
