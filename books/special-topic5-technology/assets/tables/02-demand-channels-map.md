## Demand channels map

| Channel | Core labor-economic question | Typical outcomes | Representative papers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Displacement | Which tasks/workers are substituted away? | employment declines, occupational contraction, wage pressure | [@autorLevyMurnane2003SkillContent]; [@acemogluRestrepo2020Robots] |
| Augmentation | Which workers/tasks become more productive with technology? | higher productivity, changing task mix, skill upgrading | [@autor2015Jobs]; [@brynjolfssonLiRaymond2025GenerativeAI] |
| Output / scale effects | Do productivity gains expand product demand enough to raise labor demand elsewhere? | sales growth, establishment expansion, more hiring in complementary tasks | [@autor2015Jobs]; [@aghionBergeaudBoppartKlenowLi2025differentUsesAI] |
| New-task creation | Does technology generate new occupations/tasks rather than only replacing old ones? | occupational churn, new roles, reallocation across tasks | [@acemogluRestrepo2019AutomationNewTasks]; [@deming2025technologicalDisruption] |
| Bargaining / incidence | Does technology weaken labor's share or bargaining position even if employment does not collapse? | wage decoupling, labor share changes, within-firm rent shifts | [@acemogluRestrepo2020Robots]; [@hampoleMontesOberfield2025] |
