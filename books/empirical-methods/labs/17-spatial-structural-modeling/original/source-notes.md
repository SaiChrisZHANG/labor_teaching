# Source Notes

## Teaching Anchor

The primary anchor is Monte, Redding, and Rossi-Hansberg on commuting, migration, and local employment elasticities [@monteReddingRossiHansberg2018]. The lab borrows the methodological idea that bilateral flows and spatial equilibrium jointly determine local incidence.

## Data Status

The lab uses deterministic synthetic data. It does not use confidential commuting records, proprietary routing data, or an official replication package.

## What Is Reproduced

Students reproduce a teaching-scale version of three objects:

1. a gravity-style commuting relationship;
2. commuting openness by residence location;
3. a local productivity shock interpreted through fixed-flow and equilibrium lenses.

## What Is Not Reproduced

The lab does not reproduce the published model, data cleaning, estimation, or counterfactuals from Monte, Redding, and Rossi-Hansberg. It is a controlled exercise for understanding structural objects and validation burdens.
