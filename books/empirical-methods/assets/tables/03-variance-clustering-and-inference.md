| Inference problem | Why it matters | Practical response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Serial correlation in outcomes | Naive standard errors can dramatically overreject | Cluster at the treatment or shock level and justify that choice |
| Too few clusters | Cluster-robust asymptotics can be unreliable | Use wild cluster bootstrap or complementary randomization-style inference |
| Multi-level dependence | Errors may co-move within place, firm, or state and over time | Choose the clustering level based on treatment assignment and residual dependence |
| Highly persistent outcomes | Standard error precision may be illusory | Treat long panels with extra caution and inspect placebo variation |
| Pre-trend overinterpretation | “Insignificant leads” do not prove parallel trends | Use institutional arguments and sensitivity checks, not only lead coefficients |
| Synthetic-control placebo inference | There may be no conventional asymptotic reference distribution | Use permutation/placebo comparisons and pre-fit diagnostics |
