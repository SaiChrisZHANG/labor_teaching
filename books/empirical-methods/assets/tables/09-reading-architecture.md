## Reading Architecture

| Layer | Question | Core readings | Why they matter |
|---|---|---|---|
| Foundations | What does ML contribute to applied econometrics? | Mullainathan and Spiess (2017); Belloni, Chernozhukov, and Hansen (2014) | Establishes prediction, regularization, and high-dimensional thinking from an economics perspective |
| Measurement applications | How is prediction turned into an economic variable? | Ikudo (2018); Dahlhaus et al. (2025); Turrell et al. (2019) | Shows the measurement role of prediction in labor and macro/labor data construction |
| Frontier labor applications | How do economists use modern predictive tools on labor data? | Hansen et al. (2023); Dahlhaus et al. (2025) | Connects prediction to job postings, remote work, and technology measurement |
| Research design takeaway | When should economists trust and use predicted objects? | Mullainathan and Spiess (2017); Belloni, Chernozhukov, and Hansen (2014) | Frames the lecture around use, interpretation, and limitations rather than software |
