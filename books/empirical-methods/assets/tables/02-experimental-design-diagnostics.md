:::{table} Experimental design diagnostics and interpretation checklist
:label: tbl:experimental-diagnostics
| Object | Practical question | Typical evidence | Main failure mode |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Unit of randomization | Who was actually randomized: individuals, firms, clusters, markets? | Explicit assignment protocol and design diagram | Misreading clustered or market assignment as individual treatment |
| Estimand | Am I interpreting ATE, ITT, or a complier effect? | Explicit parameter statement and take-up discussion | Slippage between assignment and receipt |
| Compliance / take-up | Did assignment meaningfully shift treatment receipt? | First stage, treatment uptake tables, implementation logs | Weak take-up and overinterpretation of receipt effects |
| Attrition / missing outcomes | Are outcome data complete and comparable? | Attrition tables, bounds, robustness to missing outcomes | Selective attrition that reintroduces selection bias |
| Spillovers / interference | Could untreated units be affected? | Cluster design, saturation design, exposure mapping | Treating a spillover-rich design as SUTVA-clean |
| Implementation fidelity | Was the treatment actually delivered as intended? | Protocol adherence, staff logs, monitoring data | “Randomized intent, messy treatment” |
| External validity / scale | Does the intervention scale to other places or market sizes? | Institutional detail, comparison to normal practice, equilibrium discussion | Extrapolating a pilot into a market-wide claim |
| Ethics and transparency | Were subjects informed, protected, and analyzed transparently? | Pre-analysis plans, disclosure, IRB/ethics notes | Design credibility under weak implementation transparency |
:::
