## Structural estimation diagnostics

| Question | What to ask | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What is latent? | Preferences, costs, productivity, beliefs, constraints, or technology? | Structural work is justified only if the latent object matters for the research question. |
| What identifies it? | Which moments, transitions, or distributions move the primitive? | Readers need to see which data variation disciplines the latent object. |
| Why this estimator? | Full likelihood, moments/GMM, SMM, or indirect inference? | Estimation choice determines what parts of the data the model is forced to explain. |
| What is targeted? | Which moments are explicitly matched? | Targeted moments define the model’s empirical obligations. |
| What is untargeted? | Which non-target moments or distributions are shown for validation? | Good validation shows the model explains more than the moments it was fit to. |
| What is the counterfactual? | Is it near the observed support or a large extrapolation? | Counterfactual credibility depends on distance from observed institutional variation. |
| How is uncertainty reported? | Hessian, sandwich, delta method, bootstrap, cluster bootstrap, simulation-aware inference? | Structural results without clear inference are hard to interpret. |
