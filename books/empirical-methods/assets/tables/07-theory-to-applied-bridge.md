## Theory-to-applied bridge

| Paper | Applied question | Why reduced-form alone is insufficient | Estimation / implementation lesson |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Rust (1987) | How do replacement costs and continuation values shape dynamic maintenance decisions? | Observed replacement hazards do not directly reveal latent costs or dynamic tradeoffs. | Likelihood-based dynamic estimation with explicit continuation values. |
| Keane and Wolpin (1997) | How do young workers choose among schooling, work, and occupations? | Career paths depend on latent skill accumulation and dynamic option value. | Simulation and state-space management become core empirical choices. |
| Todd and Wolpin (2006) | How should a schooling subsidy be evaluated out of sample? | Policy changes future choices and lifecycle paths beyond observed treatment contrasts. | Structural fit and validation using policy-experimental variation. |
| Low, Meghir, and Pistaferri (2010) | How much insurance do households have against wage and employment risk? | Wage and employment shocks interact dynamically with savings and labor supply. | Moment choice must map directly to insurance and labor-supply primitives. |
| Adda, Dustmann, and Stevens (2017) | What are the lifecycle career costs of children? | The object of interest is dynamic, latent, and welfare-relevant. | Dynamic fit and counterfactual discipline matter more than one reduced-form estimate. |
