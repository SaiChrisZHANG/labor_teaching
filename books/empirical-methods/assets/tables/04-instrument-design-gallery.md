| Instrument family | Signature paper(s) | First-stage logic | Key exclusion threat | Practical caution |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Policy eligibility / institutional rules | [@angrist1991; @oreopoulos2006] | Rules shift schooling or treatment eligibility | Cohort/seasonality or policy co-movements affect outcomes directly | Be explicit about the policy rule and the affected compliance margin |
| Distance / cost / access | [@card1995] | Lower cost or easier access changes treatment take-up | Geography affects outcomes through local opportunity, peers, or quality | Distance instruments often require rich geographic controls and careful complier interpretation |
| Judge / caseworker leniency (leave-out) | [@autorKostolMogstadSetzler2019] | Quasi-randomly assigned decision-makers differ in propensities | Decision-makers may affect outcomes through channels other than the target treatment | Verify random assignment and use leave-one-out construction to avoid mechanical bias |
| Shift-share / Bartik | [@adaoKolesarMorales2019; @borusyakHullJaravel2022] | Common shocks hit places/sectors differently because of exposure shares | Shares may proxy endogenous structure; shocks may not be quasi-random | Inference should respect the shock structure, not only geography |
| Historical / legacy instruments | [@acemogluJohnsonRobinson2001] | Past conditions predict current treatment/institutions | Historical variables often affect outcomes through many persistent channels | Require unusually explicit mechanism arguments and strong falsification work |
| Multiple instruments | [@mogstadTorgovitskyWalters2021] | Different instruments move different treatment margins | Weighted averages may not correspond to the target causal parameter | Explain why combining instruments is substantively meaningful, not just statistically convenient |
