## Reading architecture

| Role in the lecture | Paper(s) | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dynamic likelihood benchmark | Rust (1987) | Canonical demonstration of likelihood-based dynamic structural estimation |
| Dynamic implementation shortcut | Hotz and Miller (1993) | Shows how CCP methods reduce computational burden |
| Moments + equilibrium + counterfactuals | Berry, Levinsohn, and Pakes (1995); Nevo (2000) | Canonical example of moments, simulation, fit, and counterfactual discipline |
| Validation logic | Todd and Wolpin (2006) | Shows why structural claims are stronger when validated against policy variation |
| Labor lifecycle application | Low, Meghir, and Pistaferri (2010); Adda, Dustmann, and Stevens (2017) | Maps estimation choices to labor economics questions students care about |
| Overviews and practice | Newey and McFadden (1994); Keane (2010); Aguirregabiria and Mira (2010) | Provides language for evaluation and transparent reporting |
| Inference / simulation references | Pakes and Pollard (1989); Gourieroux, Monfort, and Renault (1993); Murphy and Topel (1985) | Connects computation to uncertainty quantification |
