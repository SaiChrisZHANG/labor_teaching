### Network data concepts map

| Object | Practical definition | Economic meaning | Common pitfall |
|---|---|---|---|
| Node | Worker, firm, school, neighborhood, manager, vacancy, household | Unit whose outcomes or choices matter | Treating administratively convenient units as substantively natural |
| Edge | Referral, coworking, co-residence, collaboration, message, assignment | Channel of information, trust, monitoring, peer exposure, or bargaining | Mixing multiple mechanisms into one edge definition |
| Direction | {math}`i \to j` versus undirected link | Asymmetric recommendation, supervision, or information | Ignoring that influence may be one-sided |
| Weight | Frequency, duration, value, intensity | Strength of relationship or exposure | Treating very weak and very strong ties as equivalent |
| Bipartite graph | Worker–firm, student–school, doctor–hospital, applicant–vacancy | Assignment/attachment relation | Projecting too quickly into a one-mode network |
| Dynamic network | Links observed by period or spell | Timing of exposure, job-to-job information flow | Using a static snapshot when relationships evolve |
