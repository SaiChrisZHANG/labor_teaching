| Reading tier | Papers | Why students should read them |
|---|---|---|
| Core conceptual anchor | Manski (1993); Bramoullé, Djebbari, and Fortin (2009) | defines the reflection problem and the topology-based identification logic |
| Interference / exposure anchor | Aronow and Samii (2017); Miguel and Kremer (2004) | shows how exposure mappings and spillovers change the estimand |
| Labor application anchor | Cornelissen, Dustmann, and Schönberg (2017); Pallais and Sands (2016) | translates peer and referral mechanisms into labor-market designs |
| Network inequality / referral extension | Barwick et al. (2019) | shows how labor-network designs speak to inequality and matching efficiency |
| Methods / inference follow-up | Graham (2019); Canen (2022) | useful references when students need dyadic or network-specific inference tools |
