:::{table} Suggested lecture-2 reading architecture
:label: tbl:lecture2-reading-roles
| Role in lecture | Paper | Why it is here |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Taxonomy and framing | Harrison and List | Organizes lab, artefactual field, framed field, and natural field experiments |
| Randomized encouragement anchor | Duflo and Saez | Shows ITT, take-up, and peer spillovers in a labor-relevant setting |
| Audit/field design anchor | Bertrand and Mullainathan | Canonical signal-randomization design in hiring |
| Firm-level randomized intervention | Bloom et al. | Shows firm-level treatment, implementation, and productivity outcomes |
| Online labor-market experiment | Pallais | Connects field experiments to digital labor-market design |
| Equilibrium caution | Crépon et al. | Shows how labor-market experiments can have displacement and spillover effects |
| General framework | Imbens and Rubin | Keeps the experimental design tied to potential outcomes and estimands |
