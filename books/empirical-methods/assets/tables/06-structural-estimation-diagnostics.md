## Structural Estimation Diagnostics

| Question | Why it matters | What to look for in a paper |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What is latent? | Structure should recover something not directly observed | Preferences, search costs, discounting, expectations, switching costs, latent productivity |
| Why is reduced-form not enough? | Structure is costly and should solve a real problem | Dynamic incentives, welfare, counterfactuals outside support, equilibrium response |
| What variation disciplines the model? | Identification cannot be replaced by “the model says so” | Experimental variation, policy changes, panel transitions, exclusions, targeted moments |
| What is being fit? | Fit should be interpretable and transparent | Likelihood, moments, CCPs, simulation targets, non-target validation |
| What is the counterfactual? | Structural work should buy a disciplined new object | New policy regime, alternative incentives, dynamic response, welfare comparison |
| Where could the model fail? | Structural estimates are sensitive to misspecification | Functional form, expectations, heterogeneity, weak off-target fit, extrapolation |
