:::{table} Theory-to-applied bridge for Lecture 2
:label: tbl:exp-theory-applied-bridge
| Theoretical object | Applied translation | Typical empirical move | What readers will ask |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Random assignment | Build the counterfactual by design | Randomize treatment or encouragement | Was assignment actually random and correctly implemented? |
| ITT | Estimate the effect of being offered treatment | Compare outcomes by assignment status | Is ITT the policy-relevant object here? |
| Compliance / take-up | Treatment receipt is endogenous after assignment | Report first stage and treatment uptake | Are you overinterpreting receipt effects? |
| LATE / Wald logic | Randomized encouragement identifies complier effects | Divide reduced-form outcome effect by first stage | Who are the compliers, and why should we care? |
| Interference | Outcomes depend on others’ treatment | Cluster randomization, saturation, exposure mapping | Are spillovers contaminating or enriching the design? |
| Scale / equilibrium | Market response changes the treatment effect | Compare direct and equilibrium margins | Would a larger rollout change the result? |
