# Structural Network Models: What Is Being Modeled?

| Model family | Main economic object | Typical labor application | What the model adds |
|---|---|---|---|
| Behavior on fixed networks | Outcomes depend on observed peers/exposure | Coworker learning, referrals, peer effort | Counterfactuals holding links fixed |
| Endogenous network formation | Link creation/dissolution is strategic or selective | Referral formation, collaboration, team formation | Policies that change who connects to whom |
| Search/referral equilibrium | Information about jobs travels through links | Job search, access to vacancies, wage inequality | Link distribution and hiring jointly determine outcomes |
| Diffusion / information flow | Beliefs, technologies, or norms move along edges | Skill diffusion, adoption, workplace learning | Dynamic path of spillovers |
| Assignment / matching on networks | Feasible matches constrained by relationships | Candidate visibility, platform exposure, labor-market segregation | Counterfactual changes in network-mediated access |
