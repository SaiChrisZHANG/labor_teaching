### Data sources and network definitions

| Data source | Typical network object | Strength | Main limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Linked employer–employee data | Worker–firm bipartite graph; coworker projection | Good for labor-market attachment and referrals within firms | Weak on informal ties outside observed employers |
| Referral-program records | Directed referral graph | Clear economic interpretation of links | Only captures formal referrals |
| Survey rosters / name generators | Friendship, kinship, advice, support ties | Rich on informal relations | Missing links and recall error |
| Platform logs | Worker–job / worker–client / interaction networks | High-frequency and behavioral | Proprietary, heavily mediated by platform rules |
| School / cohort rosters | Peer and cohort networks | Clear group definition | Exposure may be coarse and assignment nonrandom |
| Neighborhood / co-residence data | Local interaction or information networks | Useful for place-based opportunity questions | Proximity is not the same as actual interaction |
| Collaboration / communication records | Professional and organizational networks | Captures information flows and hierarchy | Privacy and selective observability |
