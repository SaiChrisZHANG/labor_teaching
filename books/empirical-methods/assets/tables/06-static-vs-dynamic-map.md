## Static vs Dynamic Decision Problems

| Problem type | Typical example | What is observed | What is latent | Why structure helps |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Static discrete choice | Job choice with fixed menus | Choice among alternatives, wages, observables | Preference parameters, nonpecuniary utility, unobserved quality | Recovers utility tradeoffs and decomposes observed choices |
| Static labor supply | Hours choice under taxes/transfers | Hours, earnings, policy schedule | Preferences over consumption/leisure, heterogeneity | Maps tax changes into labor-supply counterfactuals |
| Dynamic schooling/career choice | Education vs work, occupation switching | Choices over time, wages, schooling histories | Continuation values, expectations, latent skills | Links early decisions to later outcomes and policy incentives |
| Dynamic job search | Accept/reject offers, reservation wages | Search duration, accepted wages, transitions | Search costs, offer distributions, reservation values | Recovers search frictions and policy response outside observed support |
| Lifecycle labor supply | Work, savings, retirement | Employment, wages, wealth, family events | Intertemporal preferences, insurance motives, expectations | Quantifies dynamic responses and welfare over the life cycle |
