:::{table} Equilibrium structural work: diagnostics
:label: tbl:eq-struct-diagnostics
| Question | Equilibrium object | Why structure may be needed | Main risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unemployment / vacancy incidence | Tightness, matching, wage-setting | Search frictions and vacancy creation are endogenous | Matching/bargaining assumptions do heavy lifting |
| Location and welfare incidence | Wages, rents, migration/commuting, amenities | Wage effects alone miss housing and mobility incidence | Mobility / housing closure assumptions are strong |
| Market power / pass-through | Prices, quantities, markups, firm conduct | Policy changes can alter strategic pricing and incidence | Demand and conduct are difficult to validate |
| Trade / migration / commuting flows | Bilateral flows and incidence | Adjustment runs through origin–destination frictions | Flow structure may be overinterpreted |
| General-equilibrium policy | Fixed point / welfare | Counterfactuals can lie outside observed support | Validation burden is high |
:::
