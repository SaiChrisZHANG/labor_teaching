# Practical Rules and Pitfalls

## Practical rules
1. Start from the mechanism, not the map file.
2. Choose the geography that best represents interaction, not the geography that is most convenient.
3. Preserve raw identifiers and keep a fully documented spatial-cleaning script.
4. Document the projection and the reason for using it.
5. Use travel time instead of Euclidean distance when commuting or access is the mechanism.
6. Report sensitivity to plausible alternative geographies.
7. Treat crosswalks as measurement choices, not housekeeping.
8. State clearly how confidentiality restrictions affect precision.

## Common pitfalls
- MAUP: results change with aggregation unit.
- Ecological fallacy: area averages misread as individual mechanisms.
- Endogenous geography choice after seeing the data.
- Geocoding failure concentrated in some types of places.
- Boundary changes across years.
- Exposure windows misaligned with the timing of decisions.
- Edge effects near arbitrary borders.
