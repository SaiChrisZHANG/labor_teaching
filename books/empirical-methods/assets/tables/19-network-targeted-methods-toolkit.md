| Method / design | Best use case | What it identifies | Main caveat |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intransitive-network peer design | peer-effects questions with observed graph structure | peer-effect parameters under topology-based exclusion restrictions | sensitive to network mismeasurement and remaining correlated effects |
| Exposure mapping estimands | direct and indirect treatment effects under interference | effects of treatment and exposure states | depends critically on the chosen mapping |
| Randomized saturation design | cluster/team/classroom settings with spillovers | direct effects, spillovers, and intensity effects | requires plausible cluster boundaries |
| Network / graph experiments | interventions routed through observed links | peer or diffusion effects under experimental variation | design complexity and low power if graph is sparse |
| Partial interference estimators | settings with within-cluster but not across-cluster spillovers | cluster-level direct/spillover effects | often too strong in open labor markets |
| Dyadic-robust inference | pair-level regressions with shared-unit dependence | valid asymptotic inference for dyadic models | still relies on structural assumptions about dependence |
| Randomization / permutation inference | small or highly dependent experimental settings | exact or design-based p-values under the assignment rule | requires careful alignment with the experimental protocol |
| Leave-one-out peer measures | descriptive or auxiliary peer exposure construction | reduced-form association with lower mechanical simultaneity | does not solve endogenous sorting by itself |
| Control-function / structural network approaches | endogenous network formation central to the question | parameters under a model of link formation and behavior | stronger assumptions and heavier validation burden |
