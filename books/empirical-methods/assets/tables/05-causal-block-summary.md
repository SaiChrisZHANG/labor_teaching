:::{table} Block 1 summary: comparing the core causal methods
:label: tbl:block1-summary
| Method family | Counterfactual logic | Typical SUTVA/interference vulnerability | Best scenario | Main strength | Main weakness | Rough applied-econ credibility posture* |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Selection-on-observables | Compare conditionally similar treated and untreated units | Spillovers and equilibrium responses often hidden in observables | Rich observables, clear overlap, no good threshold or instrument | Transparent and broadly usable | Vulnerable to unobserved selection | Usually below strong quasi-experimental designs |
| Experiments | Random assignment constructs the counterfactual | Spillovers/compliance/general-equilibrium effects can still matter | Feasible randomized assignment | Very strong internal validity | External validity, spillovers, take-up | Often very high when well executed |
| DID / event study / SCM | Untreated trends stand in for treated counterfactual | Spillovers, anticipation, policy diffusion, equilibrium responses | Credible untreated path or donor pool | Strong for policy and shock evaluation | Trend assumptions can fail | High when timing/comparisons are convincing |
| IV / 2SLS | Instrument-induced treatment variation | Instrument may shift untreated units or markets too | Endogenous treatment with believable instrument | Can isolate policy-relevant variation | Exclusion and interpretation are fragile | Highly variable; depends on instrument credibility |
| RD / RKD / Bunching | Local comparison around a threshold / kink / density change | Spillovers and sorting can contaminate local comparison | Meaningful threshold with credible local continuity | Often highly persuasive and transparent | Local estimand, implementation-sensitive | Often very high when threshold design is clean |

*This last column is only a heuristic. Publication quality depends on question importance, design fit, mechanism clarity, data quality, and execution, not on method labels alone.
:::
