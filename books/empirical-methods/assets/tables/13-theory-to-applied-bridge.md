## Theory-to-Applied Bridge

| Paper / setting | Modality | Validation problem | How the paper defends the measure |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hansen et al. | job-posting text | remote-work classifier may overfit language conventions | human labels, out-of-sample validation, economic plausibility checks |
| Jean et al. | satellite imagery | image features may correlate with poverty only in some places | benchmark against survey poverty data |
| Gorodnichenko et al. | audio | acoustic features may capture speaker identity rather than information | link tone measures to economically interpretable market reactions |
| Haaland et al. | open-ended responses / LLM-supported coding | coding choices can redefine the object | explicit discussion of annotation, prompting, and human review |
| Blattman et al. | self-reported survey outcomes | benchmark itself may be mismeasured | qualitative validation to diagnose survey misreporting |
