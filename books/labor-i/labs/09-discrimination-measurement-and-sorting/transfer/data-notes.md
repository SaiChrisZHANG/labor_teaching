# Data notes

## Challenge anchor

- **Article:** Kline, Patrick, Evan K. Rose, and Christopher R. Walters. 2024. *A Discrimination Report Card.* *American Economic Review* 114(8): 2472--2525.

## Optional extension anchor

- **Article:** Hurst, Erik, Yona Rubinstein, and Kazuatsu Shimizu. 2024. *Task-Based Discrimination.* *American Economic Review* 114(6): 1723--1768.

## What the transfer file contains

- a synthetic employer-level application dataset with randomized identity treatment;
- heterogeneous firm-specific discrimination wedges embedded in the data-generating process;
- enough structure to compute raw employer callback gaps, standard errors, and shrunken report-card scores;
- a clean bridge from one average treatment effect to firm heterogeneity and ranking uncertainty.

## What the transfer file is for

The transfer file is not a paper replication. It is a bounded teaching dataset that lets students see why firm-specific measurement is noisier and more delicate than a single pooled callback estimate, even when treatment is randomized.
