# Source notes

## Verified source paper

- **Article:** Bertrand, Marianne, and Sendhil Mullainathan. 2004. *Are Emily and Greg More Employable Than Lakisha and Jamal? A Field Experiment on Labor Market Discrimination.* *American Economic Review* 94(4): 991--1013.

## Why this paper fits Week 9

`@bertrandMullainathan2004` is a clean Week 9 anchor because it identifies discriminatory treatment at a sharply defined hiring-stage margin while holding resume content fixed.

## What the bounded teaching file preserves

- randomized signaled identity at the application stage;
- resume-quality differences that interact with treatment;
- firm-segment heterogeneity in callback behavior;
- enough structure to compute average and segment-specific callback gaps.

## What the bounded teaching file does not preserve

- the original resume templates, city design, and sample-construction details;
- the precise empirical estimates or inferential machinery from the published article;
- downstream hiring, wage, task, or promotion outcomes after the callback stage.

## Teaching note

The local workflow is a design-learning exercise. Students should treat the outputs as a bounded correspondence-study reproduction, not as a claim to have recreated the original paper exactly.
