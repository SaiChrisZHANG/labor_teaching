# Source notes

## Verified source paper

- **Article:** Mas, Alexandre, and Amanda Pallais. 2017. *Valuing Alternative Work Arrangements.* *American Economic Review* 107(12): 3722--3759.

## Why this paper fits Week 7

`@masPallais2017` is a clean Week 7 anchor because it values explicitly described job attributes rather than inferring willingness to pay only from observed equilibrium wage bundles.

## What the bounded teaching file preserves

- pairwise choices between job offers that vary in wages and named amenities;
- heterogeneity in worker valuation of schedule predictability, remote options, and worker-controlled flexibility;
- a money-metric willingness-to-pay interpretation through the wage coefficient;
- a clean distinction between direct amenity valuation and hedonic wage-slope inference.

## What the bounded teaching file does not preserve

- the exact sample design, survey language, or inferential strategy of the original paper;
- every attribute or institutional detail from the published experiment;
- external-validity claims beyond the bounded teaching environment.

## Teaching note

The local workflow is a design-learning exercise. Students should treat the outputs as a bounded amenity-valuation lab, not as a claim to have recreated the original paper exactly.
