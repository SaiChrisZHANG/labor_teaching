# Data notes

## Challenge anchor

- **Article:** Maestas, Nicole, Kathleen J. Mullen, David Powell, Till von Wachter, and Jeffrey Wenger. 2023. *The Value of Working Conditions in the United States and Implications for the Structure of Wages.* *American Economic Review* 113(7): 2007--2047.

## What the transfer file contains

- one synthetic accepted job per worker;
- wages plus a bundle of working conditions: predictability, remote option, low injury risk, autonomy, and commute burden;
- group heterogeneity that makes wage-only and value-adjusted rankings differ;
- enough structure to compute total job value without using proprietary microdata.

## What the transfer file is for

The transfer file is not a paper replication. It is a bounded teaching dataset that lets students ask whether wage inequality and total job-value inequality move together once broader working conditions are monetized.
