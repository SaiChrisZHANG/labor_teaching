# Source notes

## Verified source paper

- **Article:** Fehr, Ernst and Lorenz Goette. 2007. *Do Workers Work More if Wages Are High? Evidence from a Randomized Field Experiment.* *American Economic Review* 97(1): 298--317.
- **Official replication benchmark:** the paper's official replication materials as distributed through the journal/archive workflow referenced by the course.

## Why this paper fits Week 3

`@fehrGoette2007` is a clean Week 3 anchor because randomized temporary wage variation gets students directly into the distinction between short-run intertemporal substitution and broader labor-supply interpretation.

## Teaching note

The bounded teaching path included here is deliberately reduced. It preserves the logic of comparing labor supply across randomized temporary pay conditions, but it does not attempt to recreate the paper's exact data construction or institutional details. Students should therefore treat the local outputs as a design-learning exercise, not as a full replication claim.
