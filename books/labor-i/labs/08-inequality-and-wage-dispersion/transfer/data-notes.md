# Data notes

## Challenge anchors

- **Article:** Card, David, Ana Rute Cardoso, Jörg Heining, and Patrick Kline. 2018. *Firms and Labor Market Inequality: Evidence and Some Theory.* *Journal of Labor Economics* 36(S1): S13--S70.
- **Article:** Song, Jae, David J. Price, Fatih Guvenen, Nicholas Bloom, and Till von Wachter. 2019. *Firming Up Inequality.* *Quarterly Journal of Economics* 134(1): 1--50.

## What the transfer file contains

- a synthetic linked employer-employee panel with multiple years and movers;
- worker effects, firm premia, and amenity values embedded in the data-generating process;
- enough structure to compute a simple AKM-style variance accounting and a wage-versus-total-value comparison;
- a clean bridge from Week 8 firm decomposition to Week 7 job-value logic.

## What the transfer file is for

The transfer file is not a paper replication. It is a bounded teaching dataset that lets students see how worker heterogeneity, firm premia, and sorting can all matter for measured inequality even when the class does not have access to confidential administrative records.
