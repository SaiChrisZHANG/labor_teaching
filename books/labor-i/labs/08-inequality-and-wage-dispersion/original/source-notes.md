# Source notes

## Verified source paper

- **Article:** Autor, David H., Lawrence F. Katz, and Melissa S. Kearney. 2008. *Trends in U.S. Wage Inequality: Revising the Revisionists.* *Review of Economics and Statistics* 90(2): 300--323.

## Why this paper fits Week 8

`@autorKatzKearney2008` is a clean Week 8 anchor because it organizes the U.S. wage distribution through percentile gaps, the upper versus lower tails, and the distinction between broad trends and segment-specific movements.

## What the bounded teaching file preserves

- repeated-cross-section wage observations across multiple decades;
- a college premium comparison;
- percentile-gap series that distinguish upper-tail from lower-tail movement;
- enough structure to compute between-versus-within and residual inequality summaries.

## What the bounded teaching file does not preserve

- the original CPS extraction and sample-construction details;
- the precise empirical estimates or inferential machinery from the published article;
- every institutional or compositional nuance in the historical U.S. data.

## Teaching note

The local workflow is a design-learning exercise. Students should treat the outputs as a bounded inequality factbook, not as a claim to have recreated the original paper exactly.
