# Source notes

## Verified source paper

- **Article:** Oreopoulos, Philip. 2006. *Estimating Average and Local Average Treatment Effects of Education when Compulsory Schooling Laws Really Matter.* *American Economic Review* 96(1): 152--175.

## Why this paper fits Week 5

`@oreopoulos2006` is a clean Week 5 anchor because compulsory-schooling variation forces the class to separate a descriptive Mincer coefficient from an instrument-specific causal return.

## What the bounded teaching file preserves

- a compulsory-schooling instrument that shifts completed schooling;
- cohort and region heterogeneity;
- experience controls that matter for wage interpretation;
- a gap between descriptive OLS and local IV returns.

## What the bounded teaching file does not preserve

- the exact legal coding and institutional detail of the original paper;
- publication-quality standard errors or the full replication archive;
- every sample restriction or specification table from the published article.

## Teaching note

The local workflow is a design-learning exercise. Students should treat the outputs as a bounded causal-returns lab, not as a claim to have recreated the original paper exactly.
