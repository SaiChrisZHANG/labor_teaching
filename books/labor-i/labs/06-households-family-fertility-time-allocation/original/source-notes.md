# Source notes

## Verified source paper

- **Article:** Lundborg, Petter, Erik Plug, and Astrid W{\"u}rtz Rasmussen. 2017. *Can Women Have Children and a Career? IV Evidence from IVF Treatments.* *American Economic Review* 107(6): 1611--1637.

## Why this paper fits Week 6

`@lundborgPlugRasmussen2017` is a clean Week 6 anchor because IVF treatment success generates fertility variation that can be separated from descriptive parenthood gaps.

## What the bounded teaching file preserves

- a treatment-success instrument that shifts births;
- heterogeneity in baseline earnings, partner income, and prior labor supply;
- post-treatment labor outcomes on participation, hours, and earnings margins;
- a gap between descriptive parenthood differences and the local causal fertility effect.

## What the bounded teaching file does not preserve

- the administrative richness or exact medical treatment process of the original paper;
- every sample restriction, institutional detail, or inference procedure from the published article;
- long-horizon career and family dynamics beyond the bounded teaching window.

## Teaching note

The local workflow is a design-learning exercise. Students should treat the outputs as a bounded causal family-labor lab, not as a claim to have recreated the original paper exactly.
