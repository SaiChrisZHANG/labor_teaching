# Source notes

## Verified source paper

- **Article:** Daly, Mary C., and Bart Hobijn. 2017. *Composition and Aggregate Real Wage Growth*. *American Economic Review* 107(5): 349–352.
- **Official replication package repository:** AEA / openICPSR
- **Replication package DOI:** `10.3886/E113514V1`

## Why this paper fits Week 1

This paper is ideal for the opening labor module because it demonstrates a core Week 1 lesson: aggregate labor-market series often mix together price changes and composition changes. The object of interest is not “the wage” in the abstract but a measured wage series whose interpretation depends on who is employed and how the sample is constructed.

## Verified package elements

The public replication entry lists, among other items:

- `AER_PP_DalyHobijn.do`
- `CPS_Matched.dta`
- `Quarterly-Haver-Data.dta`
- `readme.pdf`

## Teaching note

The official CPS matched data file is large enough that routine course use should rely on a reduced pedagogical pathway unless students are explicitly assigned the full replication package.
