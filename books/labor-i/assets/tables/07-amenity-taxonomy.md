:::{table} Week 7 amenity taxonomy
:label: tbl:week7-amenity-taxonomy

| Amenity class | Typical examples | Why it matters for labor economics | Main empirical challenge |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Risks and physical conditions | injury risk, fatality risk, exposure, heat, noise | canonical compensating-differentials object; connects to regulation and VSL | risk mismeasurement, worker selection into risky jobs |
| Schedule and timing | predictable schedule, advance notice, night/weekend work, overtime control | affects labor supply, family compatibility, and stress | schedules are bundled with occupation and firm type |
| Location and commute | remote work, hybrid work, commute time, travel requirements | changes effective value of wages and nonwork time | many datasets omit commute burden or remote options |
| Autonomy and monitoring | worker-controlled flexibility, break autonomy, algorithmic monitoring | changes utility and often productivity | hard to distinguish autonomy from unobserved firm quality |
| Work intensity and pace | staffing ratios, pace pressure, emotional load | affects utility, turnover, and burnout | often poorly measured in wage datasets |
| Prestige and social environment | mission, coworker quality, culture, respect | affects sorting and labor-market welfare | highly subjective and difficult to compare across workers |
:::
