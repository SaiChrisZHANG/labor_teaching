:::{table} Mechanism map for labor-market inequality
:label: tbl:ineq-mechanism-map
| Mechanism | Distributional signature | Part of distribution most affected | Canonical evidence / logic |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Skill supply and demand | Rising education or skill premiums when relative demand outpaces supply | Often upper half, but can affect the full distribution | Katz-Murphy style quantity-price logic |
| Tasks / polarization | Middle compression with relatively stronger low- and high-end growth or employment reallocation | Middle and upper-middle | Task-based and routine-biased technological change |
| Institutions | Compression or decompression at the lower tail and middle through bargaining power and wage floors | Lower tail and middle | Minimum wage, unions, and wage-setting institutions |
| Firms, rents, and sorting | Larger dispersion across employers and stronger matching between high-wage workers and high-premium firms | Can reshape the entire distribution | AKM decompositions, rent-sharing, and linked employer--employee data |
| Amenities / job quality | Wage gaps may understate or overstate welfare inequality depending on sorting in nonwage attributes | Any part of the distribution; often interacts with family constraints | Week 7 job-quality logic |
:::
