:::{table} Household-model map for Week 6
:label: tbl:week6-household-model-map

| Model / design bucket | Core choice object | Typical identifying variation or test | What it is good for | Main caveat |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Unitary household model | Joint utility with pooled resources | Restrictions implied by income pooling | Clean benchmark for family labor supply | Often too restrictive when distribution factors matter |
| Collective / bargaining model | Pareto-efficient allocation with sharing rule {math}`\lambda` | Income-source shifts, distribution factors, policy incidence by recipient | Interpreting intrahousehold allocation and incidence | Requires stronger structure or richer data |
| Household production model | Market work, home production, leisure, and market substitutes | Variation in care prices, household-service prices, time prices | Understanding care constraints and outsourcing | Hard to measure home production and quality |
| Fertility-IV design | Causal effect of children on labor-market outcomes | IVF success or other plausibly exogenous fertility variation | Causal fertility effects on earnings, hours, career paths | Margin-specific and often externally narrow |
| Child-event-study design | Dynamic outcomes around first birth | Event-time variation around childbirth | Persistent child penalties and dynamic decomposition | Event-study patterns are not automatic policy counterfactuals |
| Family-policy design | Responses to childcare, school entry, leave, transfers | Reform timing, eligibility thresholds, regional access | Policy incidence on labor supply and earnings paths | Policies often shift multiple margins at once |
| Outsourcing / care-substitute design | Household reallocation when home-production substitutes get cheaper | Immigration, childcare-cost shocks, market-service availability | Linking household technology to labor supply | Local-equilibrium estimates can be context specific |

:::
