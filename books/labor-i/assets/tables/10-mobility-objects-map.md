:::{table} Mobility objects in labor economics
:label: tbl:week10-mobility-objects
| Object | Typical empirical measure | Labor interpretation | Common identification or measurement issue |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Employer-to-employer mobility | Employer-change indicator, EE flow rate, transition matrix | Reallocation across wage ladders and match qualities | Survey nonresponse, multiple-job ambiguity, timing of employer changes |
| Occupational mobility | Occupation transition matrix, upgrade/downgrade rate | Skill reallocation and task upgrading | Reclassification error, coarse occupation codes |
| Geographic migration | Move rate, distance moved, region transition, commuting expansion | Access to alternative local labor markets | Amenity confounding, housing constraints, selection into movers |
| Wage mobility | Earnings growth around employer, occupation, or place moves | Returns to movement across opportunity sets | Composition changes, anticipatory behavior |
| Intergenerational income mobility | Rank-rank slope, IGE, quintile transition matrix | Persistence of labor-market position across generations | Lifecycle bias, measurement error, sample linkage |
| Intergenerational career transmission | Occupational following, occupation transition probabilities, career-rank persistence | Transmission of labor-market opportunity through occupations and firms | Occupational coding changes, family network mechanisms |
:::
