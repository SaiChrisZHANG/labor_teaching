:::{table} Dynamic labor-supply shock-response taxonomy
:label: tbl:week3-shocks
| Shock type | Likely primary margin | Object most closely targeted | Main interpretation risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Temporary unanticipated wage shock | Intensive hours / effort timing | Frisch or intertemporal substitution response | Measured response can be damped by adjustment costs or constraints |
| Temporary anticipated tax holiday | Timing of earnings and hours | Intertemporal substitution with anticipation | Bunching or retiming can overstate broader welfare-relevant labor-supply change |
| Permanent wage change | Hours, participation, career timing | Mix of substitution and wealth effects | Cannot be interpreted as a pure Frisch elasticity |
| Lifecycle wage growth | Hours profile over age | Lifecycle timing under evolving returns to work | Human-capital accumulation and family transitions confound pure labor-supply interpretation |
| One-time fixed work-cost shock | Participation / entry-exit | Extensive-margin threshold response | Persistence after the shock may reflect hysteresis or state dependence |
:::
