:::{table} Week 12 behavioral labor identification map
:label: tbl:week12-identification-map

| Design family | What it is good at identifying | Main threat if used carelessly | Good labor applications |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Field experiments | clean manipulation of incentives, gifts, reminders, or complexity | artificial tasks or limited external validity | worker effort, contract design, commitment, gift exchange |
| Survey / expectation elicitation | subjective beliefs, perceived returns, anticipated behavior | hypothetical bias and measurement error | job search expectations, perceived returns to work or training |
| Quasi-experiments with mechanism variation | behavioral channels tied to reforms, deadlines, or salience changes | confusing behavioral interpretation with standard frictions or information | benefit exhaustion, reminders, complexity reduction, identity salience |
| Structural behavioral estimation | behavioral parameters, counterfactual policy design, welfare | model dependence and sensitivity to assumptions | hyperbolic search, belief distortions, contract design, commitment |
| Cross-design triangulation | distinguishing preferences, beliefs, and attention from one another | false confidence from inconsistent evidence | any serious behavioral labor question |
:::
