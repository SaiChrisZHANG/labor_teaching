:::{table} Methods progression for mobility, migration, and intergenerational transmission
:label: tbl:week10-methods-designs
| Design family | Canonical use in this week | What it identifies best | Main threat or limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Linked employer-employee panels | Worker mobility, firm sorting, employer wage ladders | Wage changes and reallocation around employer moves | Exogenous-mobility assumptions, measurement of firm identity |
| Measurement-correction designs | Employer-to-employer transition measurement | Corrected mobility series or selection-adjusted flow measures | Dependence on nonresponse or selection-model assumptions |
| Policy reforms / border exposure | Migration and labor-market reallocation after institutional change | Reduced-form effects of migration access on wages, employment, firm outcomes | Concurrent shocks, local equilibrium responses |
| Quasi-random placement rules | Immigration or location exposure based on dispersal rules | Local effects of migration shocks with plausibly exogenous placement | Remaining selection and spillovers |
| Lottery or credit-access designs | Mobility frictions and moving constraints | Causal impact of relaxing mobility constraints | External validity, treatment-takeup margins |
| Rank-rank and transition-matrix methods | Intergenerational mobility measurement | Descriptive persistence and relative mobility patterns | Not a causal mechanism by itself |
| Occupation-based decompositions | Careers and intergenerational persistence | Contribution of occupations/careers to mobility | Descriptive decomposition may not isolate causal channels |
:::
