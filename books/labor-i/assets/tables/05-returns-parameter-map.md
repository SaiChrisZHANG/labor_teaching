:::{table} Parameter map for returns-to-schooling research
:label: tbl:returns-parameter-map
| Object | Typical empirical design | What it measures | Main interpretive threat |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Descriptive schooling premium | OLS Mincer regression | Conditional association between schooling and wages | Selection on ability, preferences, family background, and sorting |
| Average treatment effect (ATE) | Strong structural assumptions or experimental analogues | Mean causal gain from additional schooling for the target population | External validity and treatment heterogeneity |
| Local average treatment effect (LATE) | IV using compulsory-schooling or eligibility variation | Causal return for compliers at the policy margin shifted by the instrument | Margin-specific interpretation; compliers may differ across instruments |
| Marginal policy effect / MTE-style object | Structural or local instrumental-variables approaches | Return for individuals induced by a specific marginal policy change | Stronger functional-form and support requirements |
| Firm-mediated schooling return | Matched employer--employee decompositions or access-to-firm designs | Share of education premium operating through access to higher-paying firms | Firm effects may also bundle rents, amenities, and selection |
:::
