:::{table} Empirical design map for dynamic labor supply
:label: tbl:week3-designs
| Design | Key identifying variation | Object learned | Major threat |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Randomized temporary wage variation | Exogenous short-run change in pay | Intertemporal substitution / short-run effort response | External validity to broader labor-market settings |
| Panel wage-hours variation | Within-worker changes in wages and hours over time | Dynamic reduced-form hours response | Wage endogeneity, measurement error, serial correlation |
| Tax timing or tax reform design | Changes in the timing or slope of the net-of-tax schedule | Timing response and horizon-specific elasticities | Anticipation, salience, nonlinear budget-set complexity |
| Lifecycle structural model | Joint restrictions across wages, hours, savings, and family states | Lifecycle labor-supply parameters and counterfactual policy effects | Model dependence and misspecification |
| Event study around a life transition | Discrete timing of childbirth, health shock, or retirement | Persistence and transition dynamics | Concurrent shocks, selection into transition timing |
:::
