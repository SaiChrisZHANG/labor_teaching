:::{table} Inequality concepts map
:label: tbl:ineq-concepts-map
| Empirical object | What it captures | What it misses | When it is the right object |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hourly wages | Price of labor per hour worked; cleanest object for wage-setting questions | Hours, employment, nonwage amenities, household insurance | Skill prices, returns to schooling, firm premia |
| Annual earnings | Combined effect of wages, hours, weeks, and employment margins | Nonwage job quality; household sharing | Labor-market well-being when nonemployment/hours are central |
| Hours / employment | Quantity margins and participation inequality | Wage-setting unless combined with pay data | Household allocation, extensive-margin policy, labor-force attachment |
| Total compensation / job value | Wages plus amenity value, schedule quality, safety, flexibility, and stability | Hard to observe directly; requires valuation assumptions | Welfare-oriented comparisons across jobs or groups |
| Household income | Post-labor pooling, transfers, and family insurance | Individual labor-market mechanisms can be obscured | Broader living-standards or family-incidence analysis |
:::
