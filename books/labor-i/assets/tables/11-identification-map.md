:::{table} Week 11 identification map
:label: tbl:week11-identification

| Design | Typical worker-policy application | What it identifies well | Main limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Kink / bunching | EITC schedules, earnings tests, retirement incentives | local behavioral response around schedule changes | needs strong institutional mapping and may not recover broad-policy effects |
| Randomized field experiment | take-up nudges, job readiness, search assistance | causal effect of the intervention as delivered | external validity and treatment-intensity interpretation |
| Reform event study / DiD | tax-credit expansions, UI redesign, leave or care reforms | average response to policy regime changes | parallel-trends and exposure measurement concerns |
| Judge / examiner IV | disability-insurance receipt | causal effect of benefit receipt for compliers | local treatment effect and institutional specificity |
| Difference in knowledge / exposure | EITC knowledge, administrative salience | behavioral effect of policy information or effective exposure | reduced-form exposure may mix information with local context |
| Structural or sufficient-statistics approach | UI timing, policy design, welfare ranking | interpretable design trade-offs and counterfactuals | requires stronger modeling assumptions or auxiliary elasticities |
:::
