:::{table} Empirical-design map for human-capital research
:label: tbl:human-capital-design-map

| Design | Key identifying variation | Object learned | Main threat or limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Schooling subsidy / draft-lottery design | Exogenous variation in schooling incentives or eligibility | Reduced-form effect of extra schooling/investment on later outcomes | External validity; treatment margin may be narrow |
| Randomized early-childhood intervention | Random assignment of parenting, preschool, or home-visiting inputs | Causal effect of program-induced investments on skills and later outcomes | Bundled treatments; long-run mechanisms may remain unclear |
| Within-program input-composition design | Variation in center quality or input mix across treated sites | Which program inputs correlate with larger human-capital gains | Input endogeneity and multiple-testing concerns |
| Structural production-function estimation | Modeled skill dynamics and measured investments | Technology parameters, self-productivity, dynamic complementarity | Requires stronger assumptions and measurement structure |
| Credit-constraint design | Variation in aid, family resources, or borrowing environment | Whether financing frictions distort investment choices | Family background and expectations may be confounded |
:::
