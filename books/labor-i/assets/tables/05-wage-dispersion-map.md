:::{table} Conceptual sources of wage dispersion
:label: tbl:wage-dispersion-map
| Source of wage differences | Core mechanism | Typical empirical approach | Why it matters for Week 5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Worker skill and experience | Human capital and accumulated productive capacity | Wage equations, lifecycle profiles, structural accumulation models | Baseline interpretation of Mincer-style regressions |
| Selection and comparative advantage | Endogenous sorting into schooling, occupations, or sectors | Roy models, IV designs, control-function and structural selection models | Explains why descriptive premia need not equal causal returns |
| Firm wage premia | Heterogeneous pay policies, rents, bargaining, or efficiency wages | Matched employer--employee data, AKM decompositions | Education may pay partly by improving access to better firms |
| Compensating differentials | Nonwage amenities and job disamenities | Hedonic wage logic, job-attribute linked data | Wage gaps need not map directly into welfare or productivity |
| Location effects | Spatial wage premia, rents, amenities, and migration sorting | City or regional wage decompositions, spatial equilibrium designs | Some returns to skill are mediated by where workers sort |
:::
