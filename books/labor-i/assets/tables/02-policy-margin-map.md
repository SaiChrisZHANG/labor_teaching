:::{table} Policy instruments and labor-supply margins
:label: tbl:week2-policy-margin-map
| Policy instrument | Expected primary margin | Why | Major caveat |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Proportional income-tax change | Intensive margin | Changes net-of-tax reward to an extra hour | Can miss participation and avoidance channels |
| EITC expansion | Extensive margin in many populations | Increases payoff to entering work in the phase-in region | Intensive responses differ across schedule regions |
| Childcare subsidy | Extensive margin plus hours | Lowers effective fixed cost of work | Effects may depend on household bargaining and local supply |
| Work requirement / welfare reform | Participation | Changes outside option and eligibility rules | Often bundled with multiple policy changes |
| Payroll-tax change | Hours, employment, or wages depending on incidence | Changes labor cost or take-home pay | Incidence may not fall on workers one-for-one |
:::
