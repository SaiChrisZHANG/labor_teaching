:::{table} Week 11 frontier research questions
:label: tbl:week11-frontier

| Research question | Why it matters for labor economics |
| --- | --- |
| When do null policy effects reflect weak incentives versus low salience or low take-up? | because labor-market behavior depends on effective, not statutory, treatment |
| How should economists aggregate multiple response margins into welfare conclusions? | because employment, hours, search, earnings, and consumption can move differently |
| Which worker-targeted policies change future opportunities rather than just current choices? | because training, job-readiness, and family-support policies are dynamic |
| How important are heterogeneity and distributional incidence for design? | because worker constraints differ by wealth, family structure, health, and information |
| When are partial-equilibrium estimates enough, and when is broader incidence essential? | because policy may shift wages, care prices, job quality, or search conditions indirectly |
| How should administrative burden be modeled and measured? | because policy delivery itself shapes who is actually treated |
:::
