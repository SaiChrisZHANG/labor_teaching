:::{table} Mechanism map for discrimination research
:label: tbl:disc-mechanism-map

| Mechanism | Core idea | Main observables | Typical prediction | Main pitfall |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Taste-based discrimination | Group identity enters utility or effective labor cost | hiring, wages, promotions, segregation | prejudiced decision makers impose a group-specific wedge | competition need not eliminate the wedge at every margin |
| Statistical discrimination | Noisy signals lead employers to use group-conditioned beliefs | callbacks, wages, schooling returns, task assignment | treatment differences appear when signals are incomplete or costly | easy to confuse with omitted productivity measures |
| Identity / norms / stigma | social meaning and workplace norms affect behavior and evaluation | performance ratings, occupations, retention, promotion | identical productivity may be interpreted differently across groups | mechanisms can look like taste or belief discrimination in reduced form |
| Sorting / segmentation | groups are differentially assigned across firms, occupations, tasks, or networks | firm assignment, occupation shares, wage premia, mobility | group gaps persist even without large within-cell treatment effects | hard to separate discrimination from endogenous application or search behavior |

:::
