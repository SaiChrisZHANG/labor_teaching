:::{table} Policy-margin map for family labor supply and time allocation
:label: tbl:week6-policy-margin-map

| Policy / shock / design | Primary labor margin | Secondary margin | Typical outcome variables | Representative use in Week 6 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Childbirth / fertility shock | Participation and hours | Wage growth, occupation, firm choice | Employment, annual earnings, hourly wages | Fertility-IV and child-penalty evidence |
| Childcare access or childcare price change | Participation | Hours and re-entry timing | Maternal employment, hours, childcare use | Childcare and school-start designs |
| Public schooling entry | Participation at school-age threshold | Timing of work and household production | Maternal labor supply, expenditures on care | Gelbach-style design |
| Parental leave reform | Short-run attachment | Long-run earnings path and firm continuity | Leave take-up, earnings dynamics, job retention | Family-policy extensions |
| Income transfer to one spouse | Intra-household allocation | Labor supply, expenditure shares | Hours, spending patterns, bargaining outcomes | Pooling vs bargaining tests |
| Cheaper household services / outsourcing | Intensive margin for high-opportunity-cost spouse | Leisure and housework reallocation | Hours worked, housework, childcare time | Cortés--Tessada-style outsourcing channel |
| Social norms or relative-income constraint | Labor supply and household specialization | Marriage, divorce, occupation choice | Hours, earnings shares, division of chores | Optional norms extension |
