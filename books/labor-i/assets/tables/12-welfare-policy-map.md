:::{table} Week 12 welfare and policy-design map
:label: tbl:week12-welfare-policy-map

| Question | Positive object | Normative complication | Labor-design implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Do workers respond to incentives? | hours, effort, search, take-up, training | response does not reveal welfare if choices are behaviorally distorted | simplify contracts and separate perception from true incentives |
| Do gifts or fairness matter? | effort, reciprocity, retention, morale | welfare depends on reference points and organizational incidence | consider motivational framing and distributional consequences |
| Do commitment devices help? | take-up and persistence of desired behavior | revealed demand may understate value when workers are partially naive | allow optional commitment and test for heterogeneity |
| Does complexity change outcomes? | effort, participation, filing, contract choice | reduced response may reflect opacity rather than weak fundamentals | policy and employer design should measure effective, not statutory, incentives |
| When are welfare claims credible? | estimated behavioral parameters and counterfactual choices | welfare depends on normative benchmark, sophistication, and learning | pair reduced-form evidence with explicit assumptions or structural analysis |
:::
