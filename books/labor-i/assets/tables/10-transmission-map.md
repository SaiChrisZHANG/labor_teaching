:::{table} Channels of labor-market transmission across generations
:label: tbl:week10-transmission
| Channel | Labor-market mechanism | Observable implication | Empirical object |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Skills and education | Parents shift children’s human capital and career readiness | Higher earnings rank or faster occupational upgrading | Rank-rank slope, schooling mediation |
| Occupations and careers | Children follow parents into occupations or career ladders | Occupational following and career clustering by parental income | Occupation transition matrix, decomposition by career type |
| Firms and networks | Referrals or firm-specific access shape employer assignment | Higher-quality firm assignment among children from advantaged families | Firm-premium exposure, employer persistence |
| Places and local labor markets | Families sort into locations with different labor demand and wage ladders | Geographic variation in upward mobility and migration responses | Place-level mobility maps, migration selection patterns |
| Information and aspirations | Expectations shape search, moving, and career experimentation | Differential mobility even at similar observed skills | Search or move responses to information or counseling |
| Credit and liquidity | Mobility requires financing search, transport, relocation, or training | Relaxing liquidity constraints increases distant job access | Lottery or credit-access treatment effects |
:::
