:::{table} From recruiting theory to empirical labor research
:label: tbl:theory-empirical-bridge

| Theory object | Empirical counterpart | Useful data | Typical counterfactual |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Congestion | applicants per posting, callback backlog, queue length | platform applications, employer logs | application cap, queue/token rule |
| Recruiting intensity | wage offers, search effort, standards, vacancy yield | linked vacancy-hire data, JOLTS-style data | recruiting-policy or productivity shock |
| Directed search | applicant response to wage/amenity disclosure | job-board data, wage disclosure experiments | disclosure vs nondisclosure |
| Unraveling | offer timing, deadline compression, early acceptances | professional-market histories, offer records | centralized match or timing rule |
| Market thickness | number of simultaneous opportunities | market rosters, centralized clearinghouse data | centralized vs decentralized regime |
| Welfare / fairness | match quality, risk, access | administrative placement data, follow-up outcomes | institutional redesign |
:::
