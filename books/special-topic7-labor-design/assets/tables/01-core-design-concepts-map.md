### Core design concepts

| Object | What it means in labor markets | Why it matters empirically |
|---|---|---|
| Stability | No worker-employer pair would prefer to match outside the mechanism | Predicts whether centralized outcomes can persist without unraveling |
| Thickness | Enough participants arrive at the same decision moment | Determines whether a centralized match improves allocative quality |
| Congestion | Offers, acceptances, and rejections cannot be processed smoothly | Creates early offers, timing distortions, and inefficient acceptance behavior |
| Timing / unraveling | Transactions move earlier than the efficient clearing date | Changes career starts, information sets, and selection into opportunities |
| Strategy-proofness | One side can report preferences truthfully without strategic loss | Reduces gaming and changes the information content of observed rankings |
| Priorities / quotas | Institutional constraints on who can access which slots | Turn design into a distributional and labor-supply problem |
