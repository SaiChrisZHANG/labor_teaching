# Frontier methods box: measuring effort, commitment, and hidden objects

## 1. Output-based administrative data
Best when the job has measurable output: production logs, sales, call-center records, completion time, quality scores.

**Strength:** high-frequency, close to effort.
**Weakness:** output also reflects ability, task allocation, and teamwork.

## 2. Contract-change studies
Use a firm or platform redesign in pay, bonuses, probation, or scheduling to isolate incentive effects.

**Strength:** naturally tied to theory.
**Weakness:** often mixes incentives and sorting.

## 3. Randomized incentive experiments
Vary pay formula, framing, or contract menus experimentally.

**Strength:** strong internal validity.
**Weakness:** external validity and short horizons can be limiting.

## 4. Subjective-evaluation designs
Exploit evaluator assignment, evaluator uncertainty, or changes in rating rules.

**Strength:** useful for influence activities and hidden multitask effort.
**Weakness:** organizational objectives are often hard to observe directly.

## 5. Surveys linked to admin records
Link worker beliefs, preferences, stress, or commitment demand to administrative productivity or tenure.

**Strength:** lets researchers measure hidden objects more directly.
**Weakness:** survey nonresponse and social desirability bias.

## 6. Digital traces / time-use / attendance
Use badge swipes, keystrokes, platform activity, timestamps, or attendance systems.

**Strength:** helpful for latent effort timing and discipline.
**Weakness:** not all activity is productive activity.

## 7. Structural estimation
Estimate latent effort, type, or commitment parameters from observed behavior and contract choice.

**Strength:** can recover hidden objects not directly observed.
**Weakness:** depends strongly on modeling assumptions.
