### Professional labor markets as anchor settings

| Market | Design problem | Why it is a labor-economics setting |
|---|---|---|
| Medical residency | Stable many-to-one matching with couples, priorities, and timing | Entry into physician labor markets affects careers, staffing, wages, and geography |
| Clinical psychologists | Bottlenecks and early contracting in decentralized timing | Professional labor market with congestion and delayed information |
| Gastroenterology / specialist fellowships | Early offers and unraveling | Specialized labor markets where timing rules affect efficient allocation |
| Judicial clerkships | Informal timing norms and exploding offers | Entry market where prestige, timing, and bargaining affect career paths |
| Rural/public-service placement | Priorities, subsidies, and geographic staffing | Design affects labor supply to underserved areas and public-sector staffing |
