## Frontier methods box

### Experimental designs especially useful here

| Method | What it identifies well | Typical weakness / caveat |
|---|---|---|
| Platform A/B tests | Direct effect of ranking, visibility, or recommendation rules | Often limited to short-run outcomes |
| Randomized information disclosure | Employer and worker response to ratings, wages, or histories | May bundle learning and sorting |
| Randomized steering / guarantees | How platform endorsement affects trust and allocation | External validity may depend on platform context |
| Wage-floor / pay-rule experiments | Effect of pricing rules on posting, applications, and wages | Can induce equilibrium spillovers if large scale |
| Staggered platform rule changes | Broader incidence of transparency or governance policies | Identification depends on rollout plausibly exogenous |
| Conjoint / lab-in-the-field / survey experiments | Preferences over fairness, flexibility, and algorithmic governance | Stated choices may differ from realized behavior |

### What a good design should show

- the **specific platform rule** that changed,
- the **labor margin** it moved,
- the relevant **counterfactual rule**,
- and the **welfare object** for workers, firms, or the intermediary.
