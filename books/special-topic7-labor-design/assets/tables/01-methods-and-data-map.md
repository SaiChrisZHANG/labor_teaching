### Methods and data map

| Empirical setting | Common design / method | Typical data source | Main caveat |
|---|---|---|---|
| Centralized match redesign | Before/after institutional comparison, engineering analysis, welfare comparison | Administrative match records, program rosters | Other concurrent rule changes may confound effects |
| Timing reform / exploding-offer rules | Event study, lab experiment, institutional case study | Offer timing data, surveys, experiments | Timing rules may be endogenous to market stress |
| Stable matching estimation | Structural preference estimation under stability | Match outcomes, rankings if available | Strong assumptions needed to recover preferences |
| Policy analysis in matching markets | Counterfactual simulation using estimated preferences and capacities | Administrative data plus institutional rules | Counterfactual credibility depends on model discipline |
| Geographic staffing / priority rules | Quota or subsidy policy comparison in matched markets | Location assignments, staffing outcomes, worker traits | Must distinguish design effects from underlying labor supply constraints |
