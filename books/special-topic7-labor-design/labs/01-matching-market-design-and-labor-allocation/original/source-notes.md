# Source Notes

The lab is inspired by the market-design logic in Roth and Peranson on the medical residency match and Agarwal on empirical policy analysis in the medical match. It does not use confidential rank-order lists, administrative applicant files, program records, wage data, or official replication materials.

The synthetic data encode transparent mechanisms:

- centralized matching can thicken a professional entry market at one decision date;
- decentralized exploding offers can lock applicants into early assignments before better mutual matches arrive;
- program capacities and priorities affect staffing and fairness objects;
- rural-service priorities can improve placement for some goals while changing applicant-rank outcomes;
- match records alone are insufficient for welfare analysis without preferences, priorities, timing, and post-match outcomes.
