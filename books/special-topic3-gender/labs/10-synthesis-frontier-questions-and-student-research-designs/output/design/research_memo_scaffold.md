# Research Memo Scaffold: Childcare access and retention at high-premium firms

## Research Question
How does care constraints reduce feasible job continuity shape retention at high-premium firms?

## Labor-Market Object
retention at high-premium firms

## Mechanism
care constraints reduce feasible job continuity

## Comparison Group Or Counterfactual
workers near newly expanded childcare slots vs similar workers farther away

## Key Labor Margin
retention and firm sorting

## Data Source
administrative worker-firm panel linked to childcare openings

## Identification Strategy
difference-in-differences event study

## Main Threats
childcare openings may target neighborhoods with changing labor demand

## Portability And Broad Relevance
portable mechanism if care availability constrains access to high-premium firms

## Welfare Or Distributional Object
earnings, job continuity, and care-time constraints

## Contribution
This project uses difference-in-differences event study in a administrative worker-firm panel linked to childcare openings setting to identify how care constraints reduce feasible job continuity changes retention at high-premium firms, with implications for earnings, job continuity, and care-time constraints.
