# Firm-side gap mechanisms map

| Margin | Core question | Main labor outcome | Representative papers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hiring and screening | Are women filtered out at entry because of screening design, evaluation rules, or information about availability/fit? | interview advancement, hiring probability, entry job quality | [@goldinRouse2000blindAuditions] |
| Sorting into firms | Are women less likely to enter or remain in high-premium firms? | firm wage premium exposure, rent access, promotion track access | [@cardCardosoKline2016bargainingSortingGender] |
| Within-firm promotion | Conditional on entry, do firms evaluate, mentor, and promote women differently? | promotion rate, authority, leadership track, potential ratings | [@cullenPerezTruglia2023oldBoysClub]; [@bensonLiShue2026potentialPromotionGap] |
| Task allocation | Are women assigned more low-promotability work or support tasks? | visibility, promotable output, progression speed | [@babcockRecaldeVesterlundWeingart2017lowPromotabilityTasks] |
| Pay-setting and bargaining | Do firms set wages differently for women through ask wages, bargaining, or internal pay rules? | within-firm wage gap, rent share, outside-offer response | [@cardCardosoKline2016bargainingSortingGender]; [@rousseille2024askGap] |
| Retention and exit | Do workplace conditions change who stays, leaves, or avoids certain firms? | quit/separation, workplace segregation, authority path | [@folkeRickne2022sexualHarassment] |
| Firm-facing policy response | How do firms adapt to transparency or equal-pay mandates? | wage compression, disclosure effects, segregation, compliance | [@blundellDuchiniSimionTurrell2025payTransparency]; [@gentilePassaroKojimaPakzadHurson2026equalPaySimilarWork] |
