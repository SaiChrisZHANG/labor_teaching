:label: tbl:measurement-hidden-harms-map

| Problem | Why standard outcomes miss it | What researchers use instead | Key paper |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hidden disutility of unsafe jobs | Wages can rise even when welfare falls if risky/hostile jobs pay more | Job-to-job moves, quits, survey reports, workplace composition, welfare interpretation | [@folkeRickne2022] |
| Underreporting of harassment or violence | Administrative cases capture only part of true exposure | Survey-admin linkages, workplace incidence gradients, firm hiring/turnover responses | [@adamsPrasslHuttunenNixZhang2024] |
| Safety as a mobility constraint | Non-application is often invisible in standard labor data | Application data, commute offers, randomized transport access | [@garlickFieldVyborny2025] |
| Reproductive control as future-option value | Immediate wages/hours miss career timing and financial consequences | Longitudinal admin data, credit panels, legal/judicial quasi-experiments, IVF-based IV | [@millerWherryFoster2023]; [@lundborgPlugRasmussen2017] |
