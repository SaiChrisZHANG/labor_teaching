:::{table} Early choices vs later adjustment: two distinct channels
:label: tbl:week3-two-channels

| Channel | Main object | Typical empirical margin | Representative examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early-choice channel | school track, major, training, aspirations, expected fit, competitiveness, role-model exposure, anticipated discrimination | field of study, course taking, major declarations, internship preferences, first occupation | female role models in economics; competition and track choice; teacher bias and field choice; anticipated discrimination and major choice |
| Later-adjustment / inertia channel | persistence of early placement, flexibility constraints, switching costs, promotion bottlenecks, direct barriers to moving out of early tracks | job switching, wage growth, promotion, specialty retention, occupational persistence | early-career sorting of physicians; persistent career-family tradeoffs; young professional career dynamics |
| Interaction channel | anticipated future constraints shaping early choices; early choices magnifying later adjustment frictions | sequence of education -> first job -> later mobility | students choose "family-compatible" paths in anticipation of later constraints; early bad matches become more costly when later adjustment is constrained |
