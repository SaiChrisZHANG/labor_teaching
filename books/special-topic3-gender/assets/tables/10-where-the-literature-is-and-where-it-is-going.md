:::{table} Where the literature is and where it is going
:label: tbl:gender-literature-where-next

| Area | What is now well established | Where frontier questions remain |
|---|---|---|
| Households and care | Child penalties, fertility timing, household allocation, care constraints | Dynamic couple adjustment, hidden care burdens, long-run welfare, cross-country portability |
| Education and sorting | Aspirations, field choice, early sorting, role models, expectations | Skill formation over the life cycle, within-field divergence, switching frictions, AI-era task selection |
| Firms and authority | Hiring, promotions, pay-setting, transparency, managerial influence | Job design, informal authority, assignment of low-promotability tasks, evaluation systems |
| Norms and institutions | Importance of norms, bargaining, legal rules, local culture | Distinguishing norms from constraints, dynamic norm change, organizational norm transmission |
| Safety and autonomy | Safety, harassment, violence, reproductive autonomy, mobility constraints | Hidden harms, underreporting, welfare costs, long-run career effects |
| Comparative/global | Major role of development, law, care regimes, informality, migration | Portability of mechanisms, state capacity, global production, institutional complementarities |
| Measurement and methods | Administrative panels, audits, event studies, text, job postings | Gender identity measurement, intersectional heterogeneity, privacy, climate measurement, small-cell inference |

:::
