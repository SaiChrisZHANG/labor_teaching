| Topic from earlier weeks | Main empirical challenge | Why current evidence can still be weak | Contribution opportunities |
|---|---|---|---|
| Household allocation and fertility | Endogenous timing and anticipatory behavior | Fertility, marriage, childcare, and labor supply co-evolve | Better timing designs, linked time-use/admin data, welfare objects beyond earnings |
| Education and occupational sorting | Sorting vs constraint vs anticipation of treatment | Early choices embed beliefs, expected discrimination, and family expectations | Better expectations data, teacher/peer/role-model designs, dynamic adjustment evidence |
| Firm-side gender gaps | Worker sorting vs firm treatment | Firms differ in jobs, rents, managers, tasks, and flexibility | New personnel/admin data, explicit supervisor chains, internal job ladders |
| Norms and bargaining | Norms rarely observed directly | Studies often infer norms from outcomes or surveys with limited external validity | Better norm measurement, repeated belief modules, designs around norm shocks |
| Policy incidence | Firm adaptation and worker selection | Reforms move many margins simultaneously | Decompositions of incidence, compliance, sorting, and hidden costs |
| Safety, violence, and reproductive autonomy | Underreporting and invisible welfare losses | Wage/employment outcomes miss fear, coercion, mobility limits, and psychological harms | Linked survey-admin data, complaint vs prevalence designs, revealed-avoidance measures |
| Comparative/global evidence | Portability and setting specificity | Country-specific studies often understate what is portable and what is not | Mechanism-first comparative framing, replication across institutional taxonomies |
| Identity and workplace climate | Small cells and legal/lived category mismatch | Data often record legal sex but not lived treatment or climate | Better identity measurement, privacy-safe linkage, climate proxies tied to labor outcomes |
