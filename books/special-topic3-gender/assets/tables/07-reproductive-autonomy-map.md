:::{table} Reproductive autonomy as a labor-market timing and access margin
:label: tbl:reproductive-autonomy-map

| Margin | Why it is a labor-economics object | Main outcomes | Representative evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Birth control / contraception access | Changes expected timing of fertility and career investment | Schooling, occupation, marriage timing, career commitment | [@goldinKatzPowerPill2002] |
| Abortion access / denial | Changes control over whether and when pregnancy affects work and care | Labor-force participation, earnings paths, financial distress, family structure | [@millerWherryFoster2023]; [@londonoVelezSaravia2025] |
| Fertility treatment / IVF access | Changes ability to separate fertility timing from career timing and provides identification leverage | Career trajectories, hours, earnings, motherhood timing | [@lundborgPlugRasmussen2017] |

:::
