| Measurement object | Why it matters for gender-and-labor research | Common problem | Good researcher habit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legal sex / administrative category | Often the only variable in admin records | May not match identity, presentation, or treatment | State clearly what is measured and what is not |
| Self-identified gender | Relevant for lived identity and self-reported experience | Small cells, disclosure, nonresponse, changing categories | Be explicit about sampling and confidentiality |
| Perceived gender by employers/coworkers | Often the mechanism in hiring, promotion, or harassment | Rarely observed directly | Use designs where signals are manipulated or perception is proxied carefully |
| Workplace climate / culture | Can affect retention, authority, and welfare | Usually measured imperfectly and selectively | Treat climate measures as objects in their own right, not just controls |
| Intersectional categories | Necessary for realistic heterogeneity | Sparse cells, disclosure risk, precision limits | Pre-specify aggregation rules and honest limits to precision |
| Cross-time comparability | Gender categories and administrative forms evolve | Apparent trends may reflect classification changes | Document classification changes and sensitivity checks |
