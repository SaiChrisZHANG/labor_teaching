:::{table} Interdisciplinary directions for gender-and-labor research
:label: tbl:gender-interdisciplinary-directions

| Direction | Core labor question | Why this is promising |
|---|---|---|
| Urban / spatial | How do commuting, housing, neighborhood safety, and local amenities shape gendered labor-market access? | Connects location choice to job access, mobility, and hidden constraints |
| Political economy | How do representation, voice, public employment, legal reform, and enforcement shape gendered labor outcomes? | Links gender gaps to institutions, governance, and policy transmission |
| Health and reproduction | How do contraception, abortion access, IVF, menopause, menstrual health, and health shocks affect careers and welfare? | High policy relevance and strong labor-supply / timing margins |
| Technology and AI | How do task automation, scheduling software, monitoring, and AI exposure affect gendered work? | Rapidly changing workplace organization and skill demand |
| Crime and safety | How do violence, harassment, and insecurity reshape feasible sets, sorting, and welfare? | Brings hidden costs of work into labor analysis |
| Organizations and management | How do leadership, mentoring, culture, and evaluation rules produce authority gaps? | Central to within-firm inequality and promotion pipelines |

:::
