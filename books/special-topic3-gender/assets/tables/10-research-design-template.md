:::{table} Research design template
:label: tbl:gender-research-design-template

| Step | Question to answer | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Labor object | What labor-market outcome is central: employment, hours, earnings, authority, safety, retention, benefits, mobility, welfare? | Keeps the project labor-focused rather than topic-only |
| Mechanism | Through what mechanism does gender matter: care, sorting, pay-setting, norms, safety, legal protection, identity, information? | Prevents descriptive gap-chasing |
| Margin | Which worker, firm, household, or policy margin is actually moving? | Clarifies interpretation |
| Counterfactual | What is the comparison group or counterfactual? | Anchors causal or structural logic |
| Data | Which data reveal the mechanism best? | Matches question to observability |
| Design | What empirical strategy identifies the mechanism? | Distinguishes persuasion from credible inference |
| Welfare object | Why does this matter for welfare or distribution? | Converts a gap into an economics question |
| Contribution | What is new relative to the existing literature? | Forces students to define value added |

:::
