| Challenge / opportunity | Why it matters | Promising design directions |
|---|---|---|
| Defining the population | Sexual orientation, gender identity, and disclosure are distinct | Direct-identity surveys; linked admin records; triangulation across data |
| Hidden harms | Wages miss climate, concealment, harassment, and stress | Linked worker surveys + admin outcomes; climate modules; panel wellbeing data |
| Legal protections vs actual treatment | Law on the books may not equal workplace change | Difference-in-differences/event studies around legal change + firm heterogeneity |
| Hiring vs within-job treatment | Access and treatment may move differently | Pair audit/correspondence evidence with firm or payroll data |
| Small cells and privacy | Especially acute for trans and nonbinary workers | Administrative linkage, repeated panels, privacy-preserving aggregation |
| External validity | Most evidence comes from a small set of countries and sectors | Cross-country comparative work; public-sector vs private-sector contrasts |
| Intersectionality | LGBTQ+ gaps interact with race, class, migration, disability | Designs with enough power for heterogeneity; employer-level audits |
| Benefits and family institutions | Often treated as background rather than labor institutions | Benefit policy studies; marriage/partnership recognition; insurance incidence |
