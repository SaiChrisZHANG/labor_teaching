| Research opening | Why it matters | What could make it publishable |
|---|---|---|
| Hidden harms beyond wages and employment | Gender inequality is often missed in standard labor outcomes | A design that connects safety, climate, or bargaining harms to labor-market behavior and welfare |
| Better firm-side gender measurement | Many papers still stop at worker outcomes | Linked personnel and payroll data that isolate evaluation, task assignment, and promotion |
| Identity and treatment mismatch | Legal categories and lived treatment diverge | Measurement strategy that clarifies which identity concept matters for the mechanism |
| Search and application behavior | We now observe more of the pre-match process | Administrative or platform data plus a clean source of variation in choice sets or signals |
| Comparative portability | Country-specific designs are plentiful but often poorly framed | A mechanism-first comparison showing what transports across institutional settings |
| Intersectional gender research | Important but empirically difficult | Privacy-safe design with transparent aggregation and interpretation rules |
| Policy as identification of deeper mechanisms | Policies can reveal constraints, bargaining, or discrimination rather than just close gaps | Clear decomposition of which margin moves and what that implies about mechanism |
