# Hiring, promotion, and pay-setting map

| Domain | Clean empirical question | Margin observed | Main identification logic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hiring | Does identity-blinding or job-screen redesign change women’s chances at entry? | callback / advancement / hire | procedural change in screening or audit-style comparison |
| Promotion | Are women evaluated differently despite similar performance? | ratings, promotion, managerial track entry | personnel records with objective performance + subjective ratings |
| Networks and supervision | Do manager ties and informal social access affect promotions and wage growth? | promotion, wage growth, leadership access | quasi-random manager rotation / reassignment |
| Task allocation | Are women disproportionately allocated low-promotability work? | low-value tasks, committee/service load, time use | experimental assignment / within-organization task requests |
| Pay-setting | Do women receive lower firm-specific premiums or bargain differently within the same firm? | wage premium, rent-sharing, response to surplus | matched employer-employee decomposition + surplus proxies |
| Policy design | Do firm-facing reforms change gaps through pay compression, transparency, or workforce re-sorting? | within-firm gap, pay disclosure, segregation | firm-policy reform or mandate with treated vs untreated units |
