### Table. Identification, child penalties, and dynamic effects

| Empirical setting | Main object identified | Typical method | Main limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Birth event-study | Descriptive child penalties | Event study / panel FE | Not a causal fertility estimate by itself |
| IVF / infertility variation | Causal fertility effect | IV / quasi-experimental fertility design | Local to treatment margin |
| Twin / sex-composition fertility shocks | Causal fertility effect on labor supply | IV | Interpretation depends on instrument margin |
| Childcare subsidy or childcare-law reform | Constraint relaxation / policy effect | RCT / DID / policy evaluation | Often bundles supply-side and household reallocation channels |
| Resource-control shift | Bargaining power effect | Reduced form / structural collective model | Requires careful mapping from resource shift to power |
| Time-use reallocation evidence | Care vs market-work substitution | Descriptive / panel / reduced form | Often weak on long-run wage dynamics |
