### Table. Household allocation and care-work objects

| Object | Core labor-economics question | Main margins | Canonical empirical objects |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Labor supply in the household | How does market work interact with unpaid care? | Participation, hours, timing | Time use, employment, hours |
| Household production | How do households transform time into care/home output? | Care time, housework, substitution | Time diaries, childcare use |
| Fertility and timing | How does childbirth change labor-market trajectories? | Dynamic earnings, participation, occupation, firm choice | Event studies around first birth |
| Bargaining and control over resources | Who controls resources and how does that affect work/care choices? | Labor supply, expenditures, transfers, time allocation | Nonlabor income shocks, collective models |
| Childcare constraints | How do prices and availability shape feasible work? | Participation, hours, earnings, job quality | Childcare reforms, experiments, quasi-experiments |
| Specialization | How are work and care allocated across partners? | Relative earnings, home production, job choice | Within-household comparisons |
