**Mechanisms and measurement: how the answer changes with the question**

| Mechanism class | Typical labor outcomes | Key measurement choices | Common mistake to avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Labor supply / time allocation | Participation, hours, career interruptions | Extensive vs intensive margin, calendar time vs lifecycle time | Treating a cross-section as a stable behavioral relationship |
| Care and family formation | Child penalties, schedule flexibility, job exits | Event time around birth or caregiving shocks, household status | Interpreting post-child gaps as generic “preferences” without dynamics |
| Human capital / aspirations | Schooling, field choice, training, occupation | Pre-market vs post-market timing, aspirations vs realized choices | Treating early sorting as exogenous background |
| Firm-side pay-setting | Wages, bonuses, raises, promotions | Worker-firm assignment, internal labor markets, authority measures | Calling every pay gap “discrimination” without firm context |
| Occupational / firm sorting | Industry, task mix, firm wage premia | Occupation definitions, firm fixed effects, mobility patterns | Collapsing sorting into an unexplained residual |
| Institutions / law / policy | Employment, legal access, protections, bargaining | Country-year variation, legal timing, policy eligibility | Using institutional measures without explaining the labor channel |
| Norms / identity / safety | Job choice, search, commuting, retention, welfare | Perceived acceptability, risk, household bargaining, exposure | Treating these as “soft variables” instead of labor mechanisms |
