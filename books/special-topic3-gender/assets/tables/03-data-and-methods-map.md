:::{table} Data and methods commonly used in this literature
:label: tbl:week3-data-methods

| Empirical setting | Typical data | Common econometric approach | What it is good for |
|---|---|---|---|
| role-model or information intervention | course enrollment, survey beliefs, major choice, administrative education records | randomized field experiment, reduced-form treatment effects | isolating choice shifts before labor-market entry |
| experimental competitiveness linked to later outcomes | lab measure plus administrative/school follow-up | experimental treatment or measured trait linked to future choices | connecting preferences/behavioral traits to later sorting |
| teacher-bias / grading environment | school records, exam scores, university applications | teacher fixed effects, within-student comparisons, quasi-experimental school designs | identifying distorted signals and subsequent field choice |
| anticipated-discrimination surveys | expectations survey plus intended/realized choices | beliefs measurement, mediation, reduced-form choice models | separating expected treatment from realized treatment |
| early-career assignment | administrative career histories, specialty/job trajectories, family outcomes | lottery / quasi-random assignment, event-study, long panel follow-up | identifying later-adjustment / inertia channel |
| lifecycle professional panels | wages, promotions, hours, occupation, fertility, employer | panel decomposition, event studies, dynamic wage-growth analysis | mapping early sorting into later wage and career divergence |

:::
