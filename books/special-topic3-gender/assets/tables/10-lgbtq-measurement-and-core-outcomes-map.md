| Object | Typical data source | What it identifies well | Main limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Same-sex couples in census/ACS-style data | Household roster + partner/spouse link | Partnered same-sex households, employment, hours, specialization | Misses unpartnered LGBTQ+ people; not direct identity |
| Direct sexual-orientation survey question | Survey identity module | Self-identified sexual orientation, broad employment/wage patterns | Sample size and disclosure bias |
| Direct gender-identity / sex-at-birth modules | Specialized surveys, recent national surveys | Gender identity more directly, some trans/nonbinary margins | Small cells, privacy, wording sensitivity |
| Administrative gender-marker / name-change linkage | Tax / SSA / administrative records | High-fidelity subgroup of transgender workers, panel earnings | Selective sample of people who legally/admin affirm |
| Audit / correspondence design | Resumes / applications | Hiring discrimination at callback margin | Does not observe post-hire treatment |
| Employer policy / benefit records | HR / benefits administration | Benefit design, partner coverage, policy take-up | Often employer-specific and hard to generalize |
| Climate / harassment survey modules | Worker surveys, organizational climate data | Hidden harms, disclosure, wellbeing | Hard to link to wages or long-run careers |
