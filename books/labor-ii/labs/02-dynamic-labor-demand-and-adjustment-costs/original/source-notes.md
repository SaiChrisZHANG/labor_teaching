# Source notes

## Verified source anchor

- **Article:** Dibiasi, Andreas, Heiner Mikosch, and Samad Sarferaz. 2025. *Uncertainty Shocks, Adjustment Costs, and Firm Beliefs: Evidence from a Representative Survey*. *American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics* 17(3): 36--73.

## Why this paper fits Week 2

This paper is a strong anchor for Week 2 because it keeps three ideas together: firms have a target, firms face frictions when moving toward that target, and uncertainty can slow the observed response even when demand conditions have changed. That makes it ideal for teaching the difference between target employment, actual employment, and the timing of adjustment.

## Teaching note

The local lab path is synthetic and bounded. It preserves the logic of a dynamic-adjustment design while avoiding any confidential survey or administrative files. The pedagogical goal is to make the Week 2 objects runnable, not to ship a full paper replication.
