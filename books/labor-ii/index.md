# Labor II: Firms, Frictions, and Institutions

Labor II is the second semester of the core labor sequence. It takes the worker-side foundations from Labor I and embeds them in firm behavior, labor demand, wage-setting, search and matching frictions, labor market power, institutions, and major shocks to labor demand.

Where Labor I asks how workers choose, invest, sort, and respond to policy, Labor II asks how labor markets are organized once firms, bargaining, frictions, regulation, and aggregate adjustment matter. The course is therefore both a continuation and a reorientation: worker-side mechanisms remain important, but they are now studied inside equilibrium and institution-rich environments.

The course is designed around three layers:

- **Bridge**: intuition for firm behavior, market frictions, and institutions
- **Field Core**: canonical models, empirical designs, and equilibrium reasoning
- **Research Lab**: frontier questions, replication ideas, and new research directions
