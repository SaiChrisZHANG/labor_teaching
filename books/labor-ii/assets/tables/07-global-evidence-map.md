:::{table} Global evidence and institutional heterogeneity
:label: tbl:global-mw-map

| Setting | Institutional feature | Core finding to teach | Important adjustment margins | Why it matters for Labor II |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| United States | Mostly local/state hourly floors with staggered changes | Wage gains are robust; employment evidence depends on design, population, and outcome | Low-wage jobs, hours, hiring, turnover, prices | Canonical debate about competition vs monopsony |
| Germany / Europe | National floor with strong formal-sector coverage | Wage increases with muted aggregate employment losses but meaningful reallocation and composition effects | Hours, firm reallocation, prices, job quality | Shows that "no large headcount effect" can still mask large structural change |
| Brazil / Latin America | National or broad-based floors with large bite and varying informality | Strong distributional effects with firm and worker reallocation; employment effects often muted in formal data | Informality, inequality, firm composition, spillovers | Forces students to think beyond U.S. institutions |
| Developing / high-informality settings | Compliance and enforcement are central, not background details | Legal rules may not map one-to-one into observed wages or formal employment | Noncompliance, inspections, formalization, separations | Connects minimum wages to the broader wage-law literature |

:::
