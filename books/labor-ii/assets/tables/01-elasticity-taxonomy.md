:::{table} Labor-demand elasticity taxonomy for Week 1
:label: tbl:lii-w1-elasticity-taxonomy

| Object | What is held fixed? | Main economic content | Typical source of variation | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Conditional labor demand | Output | Substitution across inputs | Factor-price variation in a cost-minimization frame | Cleanest route to substitution elasticities |
| Total labor demand | Nothing on output | Substitution plus scale/output response | Wage, tax, or cost shocks with output adjustment | Relevant for employment incidence |
| Short-run labor demand | Some capital or organization fixed | Limited substitution margins | Very near-term shocks | Often less elastic |
| Long-run labor demand | More inputs variable | Broader substitution and reorganization | Persistent shocks or long panels | Usually more elastic |
| Own-wage elasticity | Other prices fixed | Response of labor to labor cost | Wage or payroll-tax shocks | Central labor-demand object |
| Cross-price elasticity | Labor price fixed | Co-movement with capital/other inputs | Capital-cost or task-price changes | Reveals substitutability/complementarity |
| Labor demand under rent sharing / wage-setting | Employment and wages may both move | Demand interacts with surplus division | Profit or tax shocks with wage responses | Foreshadows Weeks 5--6 |
:::
