:::{table} Effectiveness, spillovers, inequality, and welfare checklist
:label: tbl:effectiveness-spillovers-welfare-week9

| Question | What to ask | Typical empirical proxy | Common pitfall | Welfare interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Effectiveness | did the policy change behavior or only formal compliance? | take-up, reported compliance, inspections, layoffs, formality, search outcomes | treating law-on-the-books as policy exposure | effectiveness depends on enforcement, knowledge, and avoidance options |
| Spillovers | who outside the treated group is affected? | untreated workers, uncovered firms, informal sector, adjacent regions, prices | stopping at the directly treated margin | spillovers can reverse or amplify direct effects |
| Inequality | who gains, who loses, and at what part of the distribution? | wage distribution, job stability, formality, benefit receipt, reemployment quality | focusing only on mean employment | regulations can compress, stratify, or segment the labor market |
| Welfare | what benchmark and objective are being used? | worker surplus, firm profits, fiscal costs, BC/MC ratios, insurance value | equating small employment effects with small welfare effects | welfare depends on insurance, distortion, redistribution, and market power |
| External validity | what institutions make this estimate travel or fail to travel? | enforcement capacity, informality, union coverage, product market conditions | importing one-country estimates mechanically | institutional context is part of the treatment |

:::
