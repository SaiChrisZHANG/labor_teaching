:::{table} Cyclical labor-market margins: objects, data, and interpretations
:label: tbl:cyclical-margins-map

| Margin / object | Canonical empirical proxy | What it is meant to capture | Common interpretation risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Unemployment stock | U rate, unemployment level | Slack among nonemployed searchers | Confusing a stock with the underlying hazard rates |
| Vacancies | JOLTS openings, help-wanted series | Recruiting effort / labor demand | Treating all vacancies as homogeneous |
| Job-finding rate | U→E hazard | Outflow from unemployment | Ignoring participation and job-to-job channels |
| Separation rate | E→U or layoff hazard | Job destruction / job loss risk | Treating separations as exogenous and acyclical |
| Job-to-job transition rate | E→E hazard, LEHD or CPS-based mover series | Reallocation among employed workers, job ladder activity | Ignoring its role in wages and match quality |
| Matching efficiency | Residual from aggregate matching function | Ease of converting searchers and vacancies into hires | Mistaking composition/heterogeneity for structural efficiency |
| Wage cyclicality | Wage growth for incumbents or movers | Price adjustment in the labor market | Composition bias and sorting over the cycle |
| Match quality / sorting | Wage ladder, mismatch, firm premium, prior-wage composition proxies | Quality of worker-firm pairing and who gets displaced | Treating all cyclical wage movements as pure wage rigidity |
| Participation margin | E↔N, U↔N flows | Extensive-margin response beyond unemployment | Ignoring hidden slack outside U |
:::
