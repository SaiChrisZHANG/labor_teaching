:::{table} Bargaining objects and observables for Week 5
:label: tbl:bargaining-objects-week5

| Object | Definition | Why it matters | Directly observed? | Typical proxy or design | Frequent mistake |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Reservation wage | Lowest acceptable wage for a worker at a point in time | Governs acceptance decisions | Rarely | Surveys, duration models, offer histories | Treating reservation wages as identical to current outside options |
| Outside offer | Concrete offer from another employer | Can discipline current pay or trigger separation | Sometimes | Dual jobholders, matched offer data, survey offers | Confusing realized offers with the value of nonemployment |
| Value of nonemployment | Value of the worker's fallback if not employed | Primitive object in many bargaining and posting models | No | Policy shocks to benefits, local conditions, quasi-experimental nonemployment value changes | Treating UI generosity or unemployment rate as the object itself |
| Threat point | Payoff relevant during negotiation breakdown or delay | Determines bargained wage under specific protocol | No | Theoretical assumption plus institutional setting | Equating threat points mechanically with exit payoffs |
| Firm wage premium | Persistent firm-specific pay component | Connects wage-setting to firm heterogeneity and rents | Indirectly | Matched worker-firm data, AKM-style decompositions | Reading the premium as a fully structural wage-setting parameter |
| Rent-sharing / pass-through | Wage response to firm surplus or shocks | Measures how firm conditions translate into wages | Indirectly | Productivity shocks, demand shocks, policy cost shocks | Ignoring sorting and composition changes |

:::
