:::{table} Wage-setting regimes map for Week 5
:label: tbl:wage-setting-regimes-week5

| Regime | Core idea | Wage object | Main observables | Canonical papers | Common empirical challenge |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Competitive benchmark | Wage equals marginal product in an integrated market | Market-clearing wage | Worker wages and market conditions | Intro benchmark; foil for all later regimes | Hard to reconcile with persistent firm wage premia and search frictions |
| Wage posting | Firm chooses a wage anticipating recruiting and retention responses | Posted wage schedule | Offer distributions, separations, job ladders, wage dispersion | `@burdettMortensen1998`; `@lachowskaMasSaggioWoodbury2022` | Distinguishing true posting from constrained bargaining |
| Bilateral bargaining | Worker and firm split surplus after meeting | Negotiated wage | Wage responses to outside options, rejected offers, pass-through | `@hallMilgrom2008`; `@jaegerSchoeferYoungZweimueller2020` | Outside option and threat point are usually latent |
| Standardized pay | Firm or institution constrains pay by schedule, grade, or seniority | Rule-based wage cell | Pay grids, compression, within-job variance, mover selection | `@biasi2021`; `@massenkoffWilmers2023` | Rules often coexist with informal discretion |
| Discretionary pay | Managers or decentralized units adjust pay case by case | Flexible or negotiated wage | Within-job dispersion, response to offers, management practice | `@massenkoffWilmers2023`; `@caldwellHaegeleHeining2025` | Hard to observe the underlying pay protocol directly |
| Collective / coordinated setting | Sectoral, firm-level, or formal collective bargaining constrains wage outcomes | Contracted wage floor or schedule | Coverage, contract terms, spillovers, compression | `@bhullerMoeneMogstadVestad2022` | Institutions shape both wages and outside options simultaneously |
