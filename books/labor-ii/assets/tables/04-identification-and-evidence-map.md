:::{table} Identification and evidence map for Week 4
:label: tbl:identification-evidence-week4

| Empirical design | Variation used | Margin identified | Best use in this lecture | Main limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CPS-style transition accounting | Month-to-month labor-force status flows | U-E, E-U, sometimes E-E approximations | Flow decomposition and unemployment accounting | Coarse statuses and recall/misclassification issues |
| Vacancy series / Beveridge evidence | Vacancies over time or across places | Vacancy-filling and matching efficiency patterns | Matching efficiency and congestion | Composition of job seekers usually unobserved |
| Matched employer-employee administrative data | Worker moves across firms/jobs | Job ladders, sorting, churning, separations | On-the-job search and firm-quality mobility | Access and portability are hard |
| Online search/application data | Within-spell application behavior | Search intensity and hazard responses | Search effort, duration, and vacancy targeting | Coverage and representativeness |
| Field experiments / audits | Randomized callbacks or manipulated histories | Duration dependence, employer responses, perceived quality | Causal hiring penalties and search constraints | External validity and narrow occupations |
| Policy shocks (UI, search assistance, eligibility thresholds) | Rules affecting search incentives or liquidity | Reservation wages, duration, reemployment quality | Effects of unemployment and job quality | General-equilibrium spillovers and selective take-up |
