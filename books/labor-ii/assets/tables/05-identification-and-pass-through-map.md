:::{table} Identification and pass-through map for Week 5
:label: tbl:identification-pass-through-week5

| Design family | What varies | Unit of observation | Margin observed | Wage-setting question answered | Main limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Dual jobholders / revealed-preference test | Wage shocks in a secondary job or outside option | Worker-job spell | Wage response versus separation response | Posting or bargaining? | Requires specialized worker histories and strong behavioral assumptions |
| Policy or institutional reform to pay rules | Contract expiration, schedule reform, flexibility reform | Worker, establishment, or district | Wage dispersion, mobility, sorting, composition | How do pay rules shape wages and worker allocation? | Pay-rule change may affect many margins at once |
| Outside-option / nonemployment value shock | Benefits, eligibility, or fallback value changes | Worker or matched worker-firm panel | Wage pass-through, mobility, retention | How much do fallback values matter for pay? | Outside option often remains partially latent |
| Matched worker-firm data with firm shocks | Productivity, value added, or revenue shocks at firm level | Worker-firm match | Wage pass-through, firm premia, rent-sharing | How much of firm-side rents reach workers? | Sorting, match quality, and composition may confound pass-through |
| Establishment-by-occupation wage-rule data | Standardization versus discretion within workplaces | Occupation-establishment cell | Pay compression, within-cell dispersion | Are wages set by schedule or by discretion? | Detailed wage-rule data are rare |
| Survey-based bargaining evidence | Reported offers, negotiation, beliefs, attachment | Worker or firm | Self-reported protocol and perceptions | What do workers and firms say they do when setting pay? | Survey responses may not map one-to-one into structural primitives |
