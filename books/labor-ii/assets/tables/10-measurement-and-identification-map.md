:::{table} Measurement and identification map for Week 10
:label: tbl:measurement-identification-map

| Empirical design / measurement approach | Unit of observation | Margin directly observed | Margin inferred or hidden | Typical strength | Typical limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| National Beveridge curve and matching function | Aggregate quarterly or monthly time series | U, V, hires | Matching efficiency / search intensity | Clear macro object, easy replication | Residuals mix efficiency, heterogeneity, and composition |
| Stock-flow decomposition | Aggregate CPS or administrative flows | U→E, E→U, U stock | Relative contribution of inflows vs outflows | Clarifies what drives unemployment changes | Sensitive to classification and transition definitions |
| State or local cyclicality design | State, metro, or region × time | Unemployment, vacancies, wages, E→E rates | How local slack maps into labor-market margins | Useful for quasi-experimental cyclicality comparisons | Local variation may not identify aggregate equilibrium mechanisms |
| Matched employer-employee data | Worker-firm spell | Job-to-job moves, firm wage ladder, separations | Match quality, reallocation, firm-specific adjustment | Rich view of churning and job ladders | Often restricted data and harder to reproduce pedagogically |
| Incumbent vs mover wage measures | Worker-year or worker-quarter | Wage growth by mover status | Wage rigidity vs sorting/composition | Sharp link to wage-setting debates | Wage measures are very composition-sensitive |
| Beveridge-shift decomposition by groups | Labor-market segment × time | Group-specific U and V | Role of heterogeneity / mismatch | Connects efficiency debates to group composition | Requires strong assumptions on segmentation |
:::
