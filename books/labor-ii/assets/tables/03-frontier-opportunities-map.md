:::{table} Frontier opportunities map for Week 3
:label: tbl:frontier-opportunities-week3

| Frontier area | Why it matters | New data / quasi-experimental opportunities | Open research question |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Manager selection and development | Managers affect productivity, retention, and training quality | Manager reassignment, promotion thresholds, coaching programs, 360 reviews | What predicts a good manager across settings, and how transferable is manager quality? |
| Remote, hybrid, and flexible work | Work location changes monitoring, collaboration, promotion, and sorting | Staggered return-to-office policies, eligibility rules, digital collaboration traces | Which jobs and workers benefit from flexibility, and how do firms redesign careers around it? |
| Voice, communication, and internal governance | Information-sharing and worker voice may affect both productivity and job quality | Worker-representation thresholds, communication reforms, meeting data | When does worker voice improve performance, and when does it mainly redistribute rents? |
| Training inside firms | Firms invest heavily in learning and development, but evidence remains patchy | Training-platform rollouts, certification rules, HR systems, manager-level variation | How large are the returns to firm training, and who captures them? |
| Digital monitoring and algorithmic management | Technology can change supervision, autonomy, effort, and fairness | Scheduling software, algorithmic assignment, monitoring dashboards | When does algorithmic management complement managers, and when does it substitute for them? |
| Pay transparency and internal inequality | Transparency can compress wages, change morale, and affect promotion incentives | Disclosure thresholds, internal report mandates, compensation dashboards | How do transparency rules reshape within-firm inequality and incentive provision? |
| Team composition and collaboration networks | Firms increasingly manage knowledge production through teams | Randomized or quasi-random teams, messaging networks, project logs | Which complementarities are robust enough for firms to optimize team design systematically? |
| External validity and scaling | Personnel evidence often comes from one firm or occupation | Multi-firm studies, linked survey-admin data, replication across settings | Which practices generalize, and how do market conditions change treatment effects? |

:::
