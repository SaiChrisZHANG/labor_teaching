:::{table} Research-design template for Week 13
:label: tbl:research-design-template-week13

| Step | Question the student must answer | Good sign | Common failure mode |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Question | What labor-market outcome or mechanism am I trying to explain? | narrow, labor-focused, mechanism-aware question | vague “effect of X on labor market” |
| Mechanism | Through what channel should the effect operate? | one or two disciplined channels | treating all observed gaps as the same mechanism |
| Unit | What is the correct unit of treatment and observation? | worker, firm, place, or market chosen deliberately | unit of treatment and unit of outcome silently differ |
| Data | What data actually observe the margin I care about? | outcome and treatment are both observable or plausibly measured | data are convenient but do not match the mechanism |
| Design | Is this descriptive, reduced-form, or structural? | method matches the claim | over-claiming from a weak design |
| Equilibrium scope | What spillovers or uncovered margins matter? | explicit statement of what is offstage | treating direct effects as full market outcomes |
| Contribution | What does the paper add relative to existing work? | clean gap, new margin, new setting, or new method | novelty defined only by “new data” |
| Extension | What bounded next step would deepen the contribution? | feasible follow-on design or robustness path | extension is really a new dissertation |
