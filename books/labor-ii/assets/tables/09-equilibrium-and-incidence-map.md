:::{table} Equilibrium and incidence map for labor regulation
:label: tbl:equilibrium-incidence-week9

| Policy object | Worker-side response | Firm-side response | Key equilibrium channel | Outcome margins to watch | Why identification is hard |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Dismissal costs / just-cause rules | turnover, search while employed, acceptance of contract type | firing, hiring, outsourcing, capital substitution | reduced job destruction but also reduced job creation | employment, job flows, tenure, productivity, contract composition | reforms often bundle multiple legal changes and reallocation is slow |
| Standards enforcement / inspections | formalization, claim filing, hours adjustments, labor-supply reallocation | compliance, evasion, price changes, exit, informality | tighter standards can shift activity across formal and informal sectors | wages, hours, formal employment, informality, firm survival, prices | intensity of enforcement is endogenous to local conditions |
| Unemployment insurance generosity / duration | search effort, reservation wages, recall timing, take-up | layoffs, recalls, vacancy posting | crowding-in or crowding-out through search externalities and vacancy responses | nonemployment duration, reemployment wages, vacancies, untreated workers, fiscal costs | partial-equilibrium recipient estimates may miss market externalities |
| Information / transparency rules | application, bargaining, complaints, take-up of rights | disclosure, compensation compression, strategic job design | public scrutiny and benchmark updating alter wage-setting and worker sorting | wages, inequality, application patterns, complaints, compliance | treatment may operate through beliefs rather than directly observed contracts |
