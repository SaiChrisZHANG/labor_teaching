:::{table} Measurement and identification map for monopsony
:label: tbl:monopsony-measurement-identification

| Approach | Unit of observation | Identifying variation | What it identifies best | Main limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Applications or task-level wage variation | Vacancy, task, or requester | Within-platform or experimental wage changes | Recruiting / labor-supply elasticity to firm | Often narrow setting; external validity |
| Quit / separation elasticity methods | Worker--firm spell | Wage differences and mobility hazards | Dynamic monopsony through retention margins | Sensitive to job-ladder structure and unobserved offers |
| Production-side markdown estimation | Plant or firm | Production and wage moments plus structure | Wedge between MRPL and wages | Requires strong assumptions and rich data |
| Concentration proxies | Local market or market-year | Cross-market concentration differences | Descriptive link between structure and wages | Concentration may proxy power poorly |
| Merger or consolidation designs | Market, hospital, firm, occupation | Quasi-experimental shifts in employer concentration | Causal wage effect of market-structure change | Sector-specific and partial-equilibrium |
| Firm-shock / pass-through designs | Worker--firm panel | Product demand or tax shocks | Wage sensitivity to firm-side shocks | Maps imperfectly into a single monopsony parameter |
:::
