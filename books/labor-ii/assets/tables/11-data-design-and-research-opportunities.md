:::{table} Data, design, and research-opportunities map for Week 11
:label: tbl:technology-data-design-map

| Data / design family | What it measures well | Main limitation | Good research questions |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Shift-share local labor-market exposure (robots, tech shocks) | equilibrium employment and wage incidence across places | difficult to separate local spillovers from broader equilibrium channels | who bears technology shocks, and how do local markets adjust? |
| Cross-country industry panels | broad productivity and labor-share patterns | coarse worker-level margins and limited within-country detail | what is general across countries and what depends on institutions? |
| Online vacancies / occupation text | demand for skills and changing task content | hiring intent is not the same as realized employment | how do firms rewrite jobs after new technologies appear? |
| Firm adoption data | realized use of AI/technology inside firms | limited public access and selection into adoption | what kinds of technologies expand employment versus displace it? |
| Worker-level field experiments / operational logs | learning, productivity, and heterogeneity in treatment effects | often narrow setting and limited equilibrium content | when does technology help weaker workers catch up? |
| Historical text, patents, and long panels | long-run technology diffusion and occupational exposure | noisy mapping from inventions to labor-market effects | what is genuinely new about AI relative to earlier waves? |
| Public transfer opportunities for students | small occupation panels, synthetic exposure panels, public aggregate labor data | less causal than frontier papers | how can a lab isolate one margin cleanly without confidential data? |
