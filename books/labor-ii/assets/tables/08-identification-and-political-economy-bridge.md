:::{table} Identification map and political-economy bridge
:label: tbl:union-identification-political-economy-bridge

| Design family | Unit of observation | What it identifies best | Key limitation | Political-economy or institutional bridge |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Certification-election RD | Establishment / election | Local causal effect of union victory near the threshold | Local treatment effect; close elections may not generalize | Shows how organizing rules and employer opposition shape takeup |
| Matched employer-employee union designs | Worker-firm match | Payroll, employment, survival, composition, wage structure | Often requires restricted administrative data | Useful for linking bargaining to firm outcomes and worker sorting |
| Historical survey / decomposition | Worker-year or state-year | Long-run role of unions in inequality and wage compression | More structural assumptions; causal leverage often indirect | Connects deunionization to broad changes in the wage structure |
| Cross-country coverage comparisons | Worker-country-year or sector-country-year | Role of bargaining regimes, coverage premia, and compression | Institutions differ on many dimensions simultaneously | Best setting to teach the membership-versus-coverage distinction |
| Policy-shock event studies | State-year, sector-year, or country-year | Effects of bargaining-law changes, right-to-work laws, and duty-to-bargain rules | Exposure measurement and policy endogeneity | Natural bridge from labor economics to political economy |
| Descriptive CPS / Unionstats panels | State-year, sector-year, demographic cell | Coverage trends, membership decline, and organizing environments | Not causal by itself | Excellent for bounded lab extensions and replication exercises |

:::
