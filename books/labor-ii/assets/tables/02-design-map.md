:::{table} Empirical design map for Week 2
:label: tbl:design-map-week2

| Design family | Source of variation | Main observed margin | Dynamic object identified | Main limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Plant or establishment panels | Repeated firm-level outcomes over time | Employment, hours, hires, separations | Persistence, hazards, lumpiness, partial adjustment | Hard to separate true frictions from unobserved shocks |
| Payroll-tax or labor-cost reforms | Policy-induced changes in labor cost | Employment, wages, hours, profits over event time | Short-run and longer-run incidence paths | Firms may simultaneously adjust prices, capital, or workforce composition |
| Uncertainty shocks / expectation surveys | Changes in perceived uncertainty or hypothetical shocks | Expected employment, investment, production | Delay, option value of waiting, heterogeneity in costs | Expectations may not equal realized behavior |
| Structural dynamic estimation | Model-based mapping from states to choices | Firm adjustment paths | Deep cost parameters and counterfactuals | Identification depends on model discipline and state measurement |
| Event-study timing designs | Pre/post policy or shock path | Employment path by horizon | Response dynamics at different horizons | Parallel-trends assumptions may fail at long horizons |

:::
