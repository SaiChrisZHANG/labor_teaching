:::{table} Collective-bargaining concepts map
:label: tbl:collective-bargaining-concepts-map

| Object | Definition | Why it matters in this lecture | Typical data / empirical proxy | Common confusion to avoid |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Union membership | Worker reports belonging to a union | Measures organizational attachment and dues-paying membership | CPS / Unionstats membership measures | Not all covered workers are members |
| Collective-bargaining coverage | Worker is covered by a union contract whether or not they are a member | Closer to the bargaining institution than membership alone | CPS coverage measure, administrative contract coverage data | Coverage effects are not identical to membership premia |
| Union density | Share of workers who are union members in a firm, sector, region, or country | Useful for spillovers, threat effects, and political influence | CPS, Unionstats, establishment-level measures | Density is an aggregate environment, not an individual treatment |
| Bargaining regime | Institutional level at which bargaining occurs: firm-level, sectoral, multi-employer, or extension | Central for cross-country comparison and interpretation of spillovers | Country institutional data, bargaining laws, sectoral contracts | A country with low membership can still have high coverage |
| Worker voice | Ability of workers to shape wages, conditions, grievances, and governance | Broadens the lecture beyond wage premia | Contract clauses, grievance procedures, representation structures | Voice is not reducible to a single wage coefficient |
| Coverage premium | Difference in pay or conditions associated with bargaining coverage | Useful in cross-country and institutional settings | Wage regressions, harmonized microdata | Coverage premia are not automatically causal |
| Wage compression | Reduction in within-firm or within-sector wage dispersion | Key reason unions matter for inequality | Variance / quantile comparisons, AKM-type decompositions | Compression can coexist with modest average wage effects |

:::
