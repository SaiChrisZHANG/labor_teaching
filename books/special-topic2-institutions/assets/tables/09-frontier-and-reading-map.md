## Frontier and reading map

| Theme | Core reading | What students should learn | Frontier question |
|---|---|---|---|
| Comparative institutions overview | [@freeman2007LaborMarketInstitutions] | how to think comparatively without flattening institutional differences | what is the right comparative unit: country, regime, sector, or supply chain? |
| Rules on paper vs practice | [@boteroDjankovLaPortaLopezdeSilanesShleifer2004] | why legal coding is useful but incomplete | how can implementation be measured comparably across countries? |
| Institutions and shocks | [@blanchardWolfers2000] | why outcomes depend on interactions rather than one institution alone | when do institutional complementarities dominate single-policy effects? |
| Informality across settings | [@ulyssea2020InformalityReview] | why development settings change the meaning of labor protection | how do informality regimes interact with comparative institutional typologies? |
| Migration regimes | [@beerliPeriRuffnerSiegenthaler2021] | how policy regimes affect workers and firms jointly | which migration institutions matter most for labor-market integration? |
| Private transnational regulation | [@locke2013PromiseLimitsPrivatePower; @distelhorstHainmuellerLocke2017] | why governance may be transnational rather than purely national | when does private regulation complement or substitute for the state? |
| External validity and portability | [@allcott2015SiteSelectionBias; @dehejiaPopElechesSamii2015ExternalValidity] | how to make country evidence legible to outsiders | how should labor economists talk about transportability without overclaiming? |
