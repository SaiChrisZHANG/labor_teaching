:::{table} Worker-side and firm-side margins in informal labor markets
:label: tbl:worker-firm-margins-map
| Margin | Worker side | Firm side | Typical observable outcomes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Legal status | Formal contract, payroll registration, benefit coverage | Firm registration, payroll reporting, social-security compliance | Formal employment share, contract type, firm registration |
| Wage-setting | Cash wage vs total compensation, wage volatility, compensating differentials | Ability to evade payroll costs, wage markdowns, rent-sharing changes | Wage levels, benefit incidence, wage dispersion |
| Mobility | Entry into formality, job ladders, unemployment/informality transitions | Hiring composition, retention, separations, formalization of incumbents | Transition matrices, hazard rates, firm employment composition |
| Productivity / scale | Worker sorting into more productive firms | Firm size, productivity, capital/labor ratios, market access | Value added, sales, employment, productivity dispersion |
| Risk / insurance | Access to social insurance, dismissal protection, claimability of rights | Exposure to sanctions, lawsuits, audits, tax liabilities | Benefit take-up, compliance, formalization persistence |
| Contract enforcement | Ability to enforce wages, benefits, severance, tenure claims | Credibility of long-term contracts, payroll promises, employment discipline | Disputes, claims, compliance, labor turnover |
:::
