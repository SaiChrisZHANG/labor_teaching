## Portability and critique map

| Issue | Core critique | Good response |
|---|---|---|
| Bundled institutions | Many institutional changes move together | Be explicit about which mechanism is identified and which remain bundled |
| Implementation heterogeneity | Same legal rule operates differently across places | Treat implementation as part of the object, not as noise |
| Historical survivorship | Archival evidence can be selective | Explain data construction and what is missing |
| Setting specificity | Country/history may seem too narrow | Map the setting to a broader labor taxonomy and mechanism |
| External validity | Results may not travel | State portability claims with boundary conditions rather than universal claims |
| Endogenous persistence | Long-run patterns may reflect many later forces | Show why the proposed institutional channel remains plausible over time |
| Weak labor link | Institutional story is interesting, but labor margin is vague | Tie the project tightly to employment, wages, mobility, bargaining, or worker welfare |
| Normative overreach | Strong welfare claims from descriptive evidence | Keep welfare arguments disciplined and distributionally explicit |
