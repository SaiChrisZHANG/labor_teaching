:::{table} Frontier questions for identity, hierarchy, and labor institutions
:label: tbl:week6-frontier
| Frontier question | Why it matters for labor economics | Promising settings |
|---|---|---|
| When does hierarchy operate through workers versus firms? | Distinguishes exclusion, avoidance, and segmentation channels | Job take-up, hiring, referrals, promotions |
| How do category-based institutions interact with formal law and enforcement? | Links hierarchy to Weeks 2--3 directly | Informality, legal status, migrant labor, local governance |
| How should labor economists study authority and leadership gaps? | Access to supervisory roles is a core labor-market margin | Promotion ladders, firm hierarchies, public-sector careers |
| What is the right measure of protection versus exploitation across categories? | Hierarchy affects both opportunity and vulnerability | Inspections, complaint systems, gig work, migrant labor |
| How can hierarchy be studied beyond standard wage gaps? | Access, mobility, task assignment, and authority are often more informative | Search, match quality, management, occupational licensing |
| How do category-based institutions persist or change with reform? | Connects directly to later weeks on persistence and reform | Reservation policy, civil-rights reform, migration, urbanization |
