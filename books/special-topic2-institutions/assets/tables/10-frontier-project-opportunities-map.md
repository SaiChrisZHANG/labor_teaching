## Frontier project opportunities

| Frontier area | Why it is promising | Example ingredients |
|---|---|---|
| Linked historical-to-modern labor data | Lets researchers connect old institutions to current labor allocation | Historical census, archival maps, modern admin data |
| Enforcement and implementation heterogeneity | Moves beyond law on the books to law in practice | Labor inspector data, court records, local state capacity |
| Informality and contract form | Still central in many labor markets and tractable with new microdata | Payroll data, firm registration, worker transitions |
| Culture and labor norms | Informal institutions shape search, care, mobility, and occupational choice | Surveys, ancestry links, regional persistence measures |
| Hierarchy and exclusion | Durable social categories still structure labor-market access | Audit studies, networks, historical segregation, local governance |
| Comparative institutions | Cross-setting work clarifies what travels and what does not | Harmonized microdata, cross-country classifications, migration regimes |
| Global labor governance | Supply chains and private regulation create transnational labor institutions | Audit data, buyer-supplier links, certification rollouts |
| Worker voice beyond classic unions | New forms of representation, participation, and bargaining are emerging | Works councils, digital voice channels, platform governance |
