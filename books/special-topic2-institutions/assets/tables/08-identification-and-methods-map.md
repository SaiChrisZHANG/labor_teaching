:::{table} Reform settings and commonly used empirical methods
:label: tbl:identification-and-methods-map

| Reform setting | Typical design | Observed labor margin |
| --- | --- | --- |
| State/province labor-law adoption | staggered adoption DID / event study | employment, turnover, wage-setting, contract composition |
| Enforcement expansion | spatial or administrative enforcement intensity, quasi-experimental variation | formality, wages, compliance, employment |
| Legal-information intervention | randomized information / field experiment | legal knowledge, hiring, vacancy posting, take-up, worker outcomes |
| Formalization program | field experiment, policy rollout, threshold design | business registration, payroll reporting, labor demand, worker formality |
| Procedural hiring reform | before/after procedure change, event-study or experimental design | shortlist composition, hiring, promotion |
| Pay-transparency mandate | threshold DID / event study / distributional analysis | wage gaps, bargaining, promotions, pay compression |
| Administrative labor-market reform | phased rollout, regional exposure, synthetic control / DID | matching, unemployment, job-finding, wages |

:::
