## Method / philosophy box: how to frame country-specific evidence for broad interest

| Student concern | Better framing move | Why it helps |
|---|---|---|
| “My country has a special policy” | State the labor mechanism first | Readers care about the mechanism, not novelty alone |
| “This happened only in one country” | Map the country into a broader comparative taxonomy | The setting becomes one point in a comparative institutional space |
| “The setting is too idiosyncratic” | Explain what institutional feature creates clean leverage | Country specificity becomes a research advantage |
| “Why should non-experts care?” | Say which claims should travel and which should not | Honest transportability raises credibility |
| “The country has many bundled institutions” | Separate the focal mechanism from complementary institutions | This clarifies what is identified and what is contextual |
| “Institutions abroad are not comparable” | Discuss measurement comparability explicitly | Comparative work is stronger when limits are front-loaded |

### Common critiques of country-specific institutional research

- too setting-specific to travel;
- legal categories are not comparable across countries;
- institutional bundles confound the focal mechanism;
- implementation differs from legal text;
- equilibrium feedback differs across product, labor, and political markets;
- results may reflect case selection rather than a portable mechanism.
