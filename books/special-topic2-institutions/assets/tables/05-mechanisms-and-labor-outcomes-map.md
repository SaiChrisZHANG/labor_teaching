## Mechanisms and labor outcomes map

| Mechanism | Week 5 interpretation | Labor-market objects affected | Keep for Week 6? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Social approval / stigma | norms reward or punish specific labor-market actions | participation, search, wage acceptance, unemployment | No: this is core Week 5 |
| Role expectations | norms define appropriate work and family choices | specialization, care-work allocation, acceptable occupations | Mostly Week 5 |
| Acceptable-workplace set | norms shrink or expand the jobs workers view as legitimate | application behavior, occupation entry, competition | Mostly Week 5 |
| Self-promotion / networking norms | norms shape entry into leadership and informal advancement systems | promotion contests, managerial tracks, career progression | Week 5, but structural access barriers move to Week 6 |
| Community discipline | local norms police labor supply or wage acceptance | reservation wages, strikebreaking, labor supply | Week 5 |
| Durable category hierarchy | hierarchy changes access because of caste/race/ethnicity/migrant status itself | hiring, credentialing, networks, protection, authority | Reserve for Week 6 |
