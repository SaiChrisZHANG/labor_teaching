## Identification and evidence map

| Empirical setting | Main object | Typical design | What it identifies best | Representative papers |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Resume / audit study | employer beliefs and screening | randomized names, credentials, or background markers | differential treatment at first stage of hiring | [@bertrandMullainathan2004]; [@oreopoulos2011] |
| Network / caste setting | social closure and occupation persistence | linked group/community outcomes or group-specific transitions | role of networks and traditional institutions | [@munshiRosenzweig2006] |
| Contact experiment | reducibility of hierarchy through institutional interaction | randomized collaborative vs adversarial contact | how organizational design changes prejudice or integration | [@lowe2021] |
| Historical/public-rule shock | hierarchy through state action | policy change or institutional reorganization | impact of segregation or legal hierarchy in public employment | [@anejaXu2020] |
| Aggregate allocation framework | macro cost of barriers | structural or accounting decomposition | talent misallocation from group-based barriers | [@hsiehHurstJonesKlenow2019] |
| Stratification framework | persistence across domains | synthesis / conceptual framework | how intergroup inequality is actively reproduced | [@darity2022] |
