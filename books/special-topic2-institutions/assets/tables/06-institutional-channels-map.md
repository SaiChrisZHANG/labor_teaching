## Institutional channels map

| Channel | How hierarchy operates | Labor-market outcomes | Distinct from Week 5? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Employer beliefs and screening | employers differentially interpret names, credentials, accents, or category markers | callbacks, hiring, job access, wages | Yes: this is access, not acceptable conduct |
| Networks and referrals | group institutions channel information and insurance unevenly | occupation choice, migration, mobility, hiring | Yes |
| Credential recognition and occupation boundaries | some groups’ skills or credentials are discounted or blocked | occupational downgrading, promotion barriers, underemployment | Yes |
| Public rules and state action | law and government organizations reproduce or mitigate hierarchy | public jobs, enforcement, protection, segregation | Yes |
| Local governance and spatial sorting | place and local authority structure who can access jobs or protection | commuting, local hiring, safety, mobility | Yes |
| Contact and integration institutions | the nature of interaction can reinforce or weaken hierarchy | collaboration, team productivity, intergroup access | Mostly Week 6 |
