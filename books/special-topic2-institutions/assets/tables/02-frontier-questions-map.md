| Frontier question | Why it matters | Possible empirical direction |
|---|---|---|
| How should we measure **effective** labor law rather than statutory law? | moves the literature beyond laws-on-the-books indices | combine legal texts, inspections, complaints, and worker knowledge |
| When does stronger enforcement protect workers without pushing them out of formality? | central incidence question | firm-worker admin data with local inspector variation |
| How much of weak legal protection is actually a knowledge problem? | distinguishes implementation from capacity | worker-information experiments with administrative follow-up |
| Which workers and firms are invisible to enforcement institutions? | connects law to inequality and informality | heterogeneity by firm size, sector, migration status, geography |
| Can social organizations complement weak state capacity? | links institutions to worker voice and compliance | co-enforcement settings, union/NGO partnerships |
| How do courts, inspectors, and workers interact as a system? | law operates through multiple institutions together | linked case, inspection, and labor-market outcome data |
