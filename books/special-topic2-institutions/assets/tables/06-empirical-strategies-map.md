:::{table} Empirical strategies for studying hierarchy in labor settings
:label: tbl:week6-empirics
| Strategy | Core idea | What it identifies well | Main limits |
|---|---|---|---|
| Audit / correspondence study | Randomize social-category signals in applications | Hiring access and employer-side response | Mostly entry-stage outcomes |
| Field experiment on job take-up | Randomize job attributes linked to hierarchy | Worker-side labor supply / job acceptability | Often narrow to specific tasks or settings |
| Within-firm productivity design | Use team composition or worker interactions | Firm-side consequences of hierarchy | Composition may be endogenous |
| Network / community design | Compare workers across social-group environments | Role of group closure and local hierarchy | Reflection and selection problems |
| Historical / institutional variation | Use policy or long-run hierarchy exposure | Persistence of category-based institutions | Multiple historical channels |
| Stratification framework | Model intergroup inequality as institutional | Persistent access / protection / status differences | Broad framework needs sharp empirical implementation |
:::
