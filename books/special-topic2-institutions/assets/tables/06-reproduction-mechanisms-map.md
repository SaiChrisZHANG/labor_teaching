:::{table} Mechanisms reproducing hierarchy in labor markets
:label: tbl:week6-mechanisms
| Mechanism | Description | Worker-side consequences | Firm/public-side consequences |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employer beliefs / screening | Categories shape hiring or task assignment beliefs | Reduced access, job-specific labor supply responses | Statistical or institutional sorting |
| Network closure | Jobs and information flow inside category-linked networks | Segmented mobility, unequal access | Reliance on referrals, homophilous hiring |
| Credentialing / occupation boundaries | Categories affect recognized skill and occupational entry | Constrained occupational choice | Protected professions, segmented ladders |
| Community / local governance | Local institutions enforce group-based allocation | Differential protection or exploitation | Uneven enforcement, category-specific rules |
| Team production and social conflict | Group divisions alter cooperation or effort | Lower productivity or limited task access | Composition effects, supervision challenges |
| Public rules / legal status | Formal status interacts with category membership | Varied rights, bargaining power, exposure to sanctions | Different enforcement or compliance burdens |
:::
