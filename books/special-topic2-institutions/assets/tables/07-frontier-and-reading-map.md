## Frontier and reading map

| Theme | Why it matters now | Good anchor / representative papers | Research openings |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Coercion to employer power | connects labor-history institutions to modern monopsony, mobility, and bargaining | [@dell2010mita]; [@naiduYuchtman2013coercive] | measuring modern employer power as a legacy of coercive institutions |
| Reform and abolition as treatments | gives cleaner causal leverage than broad persistence claims | [@markevichZhuravskaya2018serfdom]; [@jonesSchmick2025reconstruction] | comparing labor effects of different institutional removals |
| Wars and labor reallocation | useful for labor-demand shocks, occupational openings, and inequality transitions | [@aizerBooneLlerasMuneyVogel2020wwii]; [@ferrara2023wars] | persistence of wartime labor openings by race, gender, or locality |
| Slavery and long-run inequality | broad but active frontier, especially outside the US | [@laudaresValenciaCaicedo2023tordesillas] | stronger labor mechanisms and better microdata outside canonical settings |
| Forced migration and local policy | links historical mobility shocks to later public-goods and labor-market institutions | [@chevalierEtAl2024forcedmigration] | when migration shocks change labor protection, local demand, or sorting |
| Historical methods and data | the frontier increasingly comes from better linkage, OCR, GIS, and archives | [@jonesSchmick2025reconstruction]; [@michalopoulosPapaioannou2020historical] | archive digitization, linked panel construction, labor-oriented geocoding |
| Cautionary lesson | persistence claims become weak when labor mechanism is vague | all | convert broad legacy claims into concrete labor-allocation mechanisms |
