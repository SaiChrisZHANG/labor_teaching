## Comparative regimes map

| Comparative domain | Core institutional object | Main labor margins | Representative anchor(s) | Main comparative lesson |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Welfare states / employment protection | UI generosity, dismissal protection, bargaining structure | unemployment, wage-setting, adjustment to shocks | [@blanchardWolfers2000] | Similar shocks generate different outcomes under different institutional bundles |
| Cross-country labor regulation | Employment law, collective relations law, social-security law | hiring/firing, protection, cost of formality | [@boteroDjankovLaPortaLopezdeSilanesShleifer2004] | Rules on paper are comparable only imperfectly; implementation always matters |
| Informality / development settings | registration, taxation, inspection, labor protection | formality, earnings, productivity, compliance | [@ulyssea2020InformalityReview] | Institutions shape allocation across formal and informal margins |
| Migration regimes | entry restrictions, worker rights, integration rules | foreign employment, wages, firm adjustment, matching | [@beerliPeriRuffnerSiegenthaler2021] | Migration policy is a labor-market institution, not just a border rule |
| Global production systems | private codes, audits, buyer pressure, transnational governance | working conditions, turnover, compliance, upgrading | [@locke2013PromiseLimitsPrivatePower; @distelhorstHainmuellerLocke2017] | Labor governance can be national, private, and transnational at the same time |
