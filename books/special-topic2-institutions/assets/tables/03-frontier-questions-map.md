:::{table} Frontier questions for Week 3
:label: tbl:week3-frontier-questions
| Question | Why it matters | Where the worker side enters | Where the firm side enters |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Which informal margins are easiest to move—registration, payroll reporting, benefit compliance, or contract quality? | Policy effects differ depending on what “formalization” actually means | Workers value different pieces of formality differently | Firms can partially comply and optimize along multiple margins |
| Are formal–informal wage gaps mostly segmentation, selection, or compensating differentials? | Central to welfare and mobility interpretation | Workers sort by skill, risk tolerance, and outside options | Firms sort by productivity and compliance strategy |
| When does stricter enforcement raise formalization versus shrink employment? | Core policy-incidence question | Workers may gain protection or lose opportunities | Firms may downsize, substitute, or reclassify workers |
| Does contract enforcement improve labor-market matching and productivity? | Connects legal institutions to efficiency, not only protection | Workers may be willing to enter longer or more productive matches | Firms may invest more and compete for formal workers |
| How should we measure “effective formality”? | De jure formality may differ from actual protection | Workers may have contracts without enforceable rights | Firms may register but still evade payroll or benefits |
