## Measurement and identification map

| Empirical setting | Norm object | Typical design or method | What it identifies best | Representative papers |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Inherited-culture setting | work/family norms | ancestry or second-generation design | cultural transmission net of current prices in host market | [@fernandezFogli2009] |
| Historical persistence setting | long-run role norms | historical proxy such as plough use or past female work | persistent normative differences across groups or places | [@alesinaGiulianoNunn2013] |
| Job-entry field experiment | competitiveness / acceptable workplace norms | randomized wording or compensation framing | willingness to apply under norm-salient environments | [@floryLeibbrandtList2015]; [@delfino2024] |
| Workplace networking data | self-promotion and leadership-entry norms | employer or manager-worker data on interactions/promotion | role of informal advancement norms | [@cullenPerezTruglia2023] |
| Local social environment | work / unemployment norms | panel data with regional unemployment or local norm proxies | stigma and social utility of nonemployment | [@clark2003]; [@eugsterLaliveSteinhauerZweimuller2017] |
| Market-level labor-supply experiment | acceptable wage and anti-undercutting norms | randomized wages or labor-supply offers | social suppression or discipline in labor supply | [@brezaKaurKrishnaswamy2019] |
| Reputation-preserving work decisions | religion and community norms | reduced-form or structural model of reputation and work | how community norms reshape feasible work choices | [@carvalho2013] |
