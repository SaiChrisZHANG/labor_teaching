# Identification and design map

| Design type | What varies | Main identifying object | Key risk / limitation | Good anchor |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Gift / generosity field experiment | Unexpected gifts, employer effort, monetary vs nonmonetary presentation | Reciprocity / social preference response | Surprise and salience can be confounded with kindness | [@kubeMarechalPuppe2012] |
| Multi-arm workplace field experiment | Pay rates, employer return, gifts, productivity measures | Social preferences versus standard incentives; extra work versus productivity | Interpretation depends on how outcomes are measured | [@dellaVignaListMalmendierRao2022] |
| Real-effort motivation design | Monetary and nonmonetary inducements across many arms | Relative strength of motivational channels | Often external validity to workplace settings must be argued | [@dellaVignaPope2018] |
| Performance-pay synthesis / pooled evidence | Incentive treatment across many experiments | Average and heterogeneous responsiveness | Composition and context still matter | [@bandieraFischerPratYtsma2021] |
| Loss-framing workplace experiment | Gain versus loss framing under operational incentives | Reference dependence in contracts and unintended distortions | Need broad performance metrics, not just target attainment | [@hossainList2012]; [@pierceReesJonesBlank2025] |
| Monitoring or supervision field experiment | Monitoring intensity, feedback, or managerial attention | Behavioral effect of observation and supervisory oversight | Hard to separate visibility from relational treatment | [@kelley2024monitoring] |
| Subjective-evaluation design | Supervisor ratings or evaluation discretion | Influence activities versus productive effort | Ratings may bundle true performance with evaluator bias or relationship capital | [@deJanvrySadouletSuriWang2023]; [@macleod2003subjective] |
