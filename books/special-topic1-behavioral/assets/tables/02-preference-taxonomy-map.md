:::{table} Nonstandard preference taxonomy for worker-side labor applications
:label: tbl:behavioral-pref-taxonomy
| Preference object | Short definition | Main worker-side margins | Typical empirical signatures |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Present bias | Overweights current utility relative to future selves | labor supply timing, effort, training completion, saving | dynamic inconsistency, deadline effects, underfollow-through |
| Commitment demand | Revealed demand for constraints that discipline future behavior | work arrangements, savings-plan choice, structured training | workers choose contracts or defaults that reduce future flexibility |
| Savings under self-control problems | Workers under-save relative to long-run plans in work-linked settings | payroll deduction, retirement saving, contribution behavior | inertia, strong default effects, delayed active choice |
| Loss aversion / reference dependence | Outcomes evaluated relative to a reference point, with losses looming larger than gains | target labor supply, effort, performance, disappointment responses | asymmetric responses to gains vs losses; target-driven effort or hours |
| Social preferences / reciprocity | Utility depends on fairness, generosity, reciprocity, or employer/peer outcomes | workplace effort, cooperation, morale | effort responds to gifts, fairness, or relational framing, not only wages |
:::
