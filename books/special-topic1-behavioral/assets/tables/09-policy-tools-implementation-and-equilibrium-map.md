| Policy design angle | Main labor-market setting | Typical design tools | Common econometric methods | Frontier concern |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Information / salience | EITC, claiming, UI, retirement, training | letters, personalized notices, schedule tutorials, disclosure redesign | randomized field experiments, ANCOVA, heterogeneity by baseline knowledge | information effects may interact with local knowledge and learning |
| Simplification / burden reduction | benefit take-up, application completion, recertification | shorter forms, pre-population, guided filing, assistance | field experiments, extensive-margin treatment effects, hazard/timing analysis | identifying whether the effect is hassle, confusion, trust, or salience |
| Defaults / active choice / commitment | retirement saving, payroll-linked saving, claiming deadlines | auto-enrollment, escalation, active choice, commitment devices | reduced-form comparisons, passive-choice margins, calibrated welfare | default effects do not map one-to-one into welfare |
| Dynamic learning | repeated exposure to schedules or rules | repeated mailings, statements, local information exposure | panel/event-study designs, distributed lags, dynamic treatment effects | responses may grow or decay as workers learn |
| Intermediary / equilibrium response | tax preparers, employers, counselors, local networks | menu redesign, counseling, interface changes, local diffusion | mediated-treatment designs, local-info interactions, pass-through analysis | worker-side estimates may miss organizational or market response |
