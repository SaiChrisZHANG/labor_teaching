:::{table} Identification and welfare in attention/salience/complexity designs
:label: tbl:week4-identification-welfare-map

| Design | What variation is introduced? | Best identified object | Common confounds | Welfare caution |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Information letter / tutorial | Facts or rules are directly communicated | Missing information / belief updating | Salience, trust, motivation, procedural reminders | Treatment effects need not equal welfare gains if responses are temporary or heterogeneous |
| Simplification / disclosure redesign | Choice architecture becomes easier to decode | Complexity / opacity | Simultaneous change in salience or hassle costs | A simpler design may help some workers more than others; gains depend on normative benchmark |
| Schedule reform + knowledge heterogeneity | Incentives change and knowledge differs across places/groups | Role of information in attenuating labor-supply response | Sorting, correlated sophistication, diffusion across networks | Dynamic learning complicates short-run vs long-run welfare evaluation |
| Monitored information acquisition | Reading/attention effort is directly observed or incentivized | Costly attention / endogenous information acquisition | Strategic experiment behavior | If attention is endogenous, optimal policy may change information costs rather than payoffs |
| Workplace incentive experiment | Contract complexity or opacity varies experimentally | Incentive salience / effort under opacity | Social preferences, morale, peer effects | Higher effort under opacity need not be welfare-improving for workers |
| Structural learning model | Belief evolution and choice jointly modeled | Persistent inattention vs learning dynamics | Functional-form dependence | Welfare conclusions depend on the normative interpretation of learned vs unlearned states |

:::
