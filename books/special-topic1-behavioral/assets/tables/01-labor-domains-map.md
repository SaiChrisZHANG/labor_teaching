:::{table} Labor domains where behavioral wedges naturally arise
:label: tbl:labor-domains-week1

| Labor domain | Why behavior matters here | Example research questions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Labor supply and effort | Repeated work choices, self-control problems, dynamic incentives | Do incentives create durable work habits or only temporary effort? |
| Job search and unemployment | Search is uncertain, feedback is noisy, discouragement is common | How do beliefs and motivation affect search intensity and job finding? |
| Training and human capital | Costs are immediate, gains are delayed, information is incomplete | Why do workers underinvest in training even when returns are high? |
| Workplace contracts and reciprocity | Employment relationships are social and relational, not purely anonymous | When do gift exchange, reciprocity, and fairness shape productivity? |
| Households and identity | Relative income and norms shape labor-market allocation inside the household | How do gender norms alter labor supply, specialization, and bargaining? |
| Worker-facing public policy | Complexity, salience, and take-up shape policy effectiveness | When do workers fail to respond to programs because they do not understand or notice them? |

:::
