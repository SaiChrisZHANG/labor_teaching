# Workplace behavior frontier map

| Frontier question | Why it matters | Representative anchor(s) | Research direction |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Do gifts increase productivity, extra work, or both? | Labor economists increasingly distinguish quantity from quality of effort | [@dellaVignaListMalmendierRao2022] | Better measurement of quality, multitasking, and persistence |
| When do loss-framed incentives backfire? | Stronger incentives may induce gaming or poor task allocation | [@pierceReesJonesBlank2025] | Connect behavioral framing to organizational distortions |
| Who responds to performance pay? | Contract design interacts with worker heterogeneity and inequality | [@bandieraFischerPratYtsma2021] | Sorting, inequality, and equilibrium composition effects |
| Does monitoring complement or crowd out motivation? | Firms choose both pay and monitoring; the combination matters for behavior and welfare | [@kelley2024monitoring]; [@dellaVignaPope2018] | Joint design of compensation, monitoring, and feedback |
| How much of measured workplace behavior is real productivity versus influence activity? | Subjective evaluation may redirect effort toward supervisors rather than tasks | [@deJanvrySadouletSuriWang2023]; [@macleod2003subjective] | Better identification of objective output, evaluation bias, and influence channels |
| How do digital or algorithmic monitoring systems change workplace behavior? | Modern work increasingly bundles incentives with surveillance and automated feedback | [@kelley2024monitoring] | Remote work, platform labor, algorithmic management, and privacy/welfare tradeoffs |
