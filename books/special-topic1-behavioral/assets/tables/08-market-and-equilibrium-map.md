### Market conditions and equilibrium persistence

| Market condition | Why it matters | If weak / low | If strong / high | Example paper |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Competition | disciplines exploitative menus and prices | wedges more likely to persist | exploitation harder to sustain | [@gabaixLaibson2006]; [@hastingsHortacsuSyverson2017] |
| Transparency / disclosure | determines whether workers can compare alternatives | shrouding and inattention survive | demand becomes more elastic | [@duarteHastings2012] |
| Switching costs / inertia | governs whether better alternatives matter in practice | rapid reallocation | persistent plan lock-in / low responsiveness | [@bernheimFradkinPopov2015] |
| Search frictions | slow movement across firms / plans / jobs | arbitrage is easier | biased beliefs and low-quality matches persist | [@jagerRothRoussilleSchoefer2022]; [@menzio2022] |
| Worker heterogeneity | determines who is exploited, insured, or sorted | uniform effects more plausible | segmentation and selective firm response become central | [@handelKolstad2015] |
| Labor market power | gives firms room to respond strategically to worker frictions | low scope for persistent wedges | more room for segmentation and markdown-like persistence | [@jagerRothRoussilleSchoefer2022] |
