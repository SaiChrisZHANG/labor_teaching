## Behavioral object, data, and design map

| Behavioral object | Canonical labor setting | Typical data object | Common empirical design | Main identification challenge |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Present bias / self-control | labor supply, savings, training, deadlines | repeated choices, contract menus, productivity panels | field experiment, contract variation, structural estimation | separating dynamic preferences from liquidity, fatigue, or standard discounting |
| Beliefs / expectations | job search, reservation wages, perceived competitiveness | elicited beliefs, panel surveys, linked outcomes | beliefs elicitation, information provision, validation against outcomes | measurement error, rational learning, beliefs vs realized opportunities |
| Attention / salience / complexity | nonlinear schedules, benefit take-up, opaque incentives | administrative tax-benefit records, take-up data, contract outcomes | information intervention, bunching, schedule reform, quasi-experiment | whether attenuation reflects inattention, complexity, or preference heterogeneity |
| Social preferences / reciprocity | gifts, contracts, employer generosity, team effort | real-effort output, wages, productivity | randomized field experiments, structural preference recovery | distinguishing reciprocity from selection or general morale |
| Identity / norms / fairness | pay comparisons, peer pressure, firm culture | coworker links, manager assignments, survey + admin data | peer designs, disclosure designs, firm-culture text designs | isolating treatment from sorting and norm transmission |
