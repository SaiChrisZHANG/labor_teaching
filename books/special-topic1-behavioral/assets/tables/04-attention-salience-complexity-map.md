:::{table} Attention, salience, complexity, and learning in labor
:label: tbl:week4-attention-salience-map

| Object | What is distorted? | Canonical labor margins | Typical empirical signature | Main design families |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Low salience | Relevant incentive exists but is not psychologically prominent | Hours, bunching, claiming, take-up | Underreaction to marginal incentives; stronger response when highlighted | Salience/disclosure interventions; schedule relabeling |
| Complexity / opacity | Mapping from action to payoff is hard to decode | Effort, benefit claiming, payroll-linked saving, training take-up | Mistakes persist despite nominal disclosure; responses depend on cognitive load | Simplification experiments; contract redesign; interface changes |
| Missing information | Worker lacks a fact, rule, or eligibility signal | Take-up, claiming, search, retirement timing | Large treatment effects from letters/tutorials; stronger effects for less-informed groups | Information letters; tutorials; informational RCTs |
| Endogenous information acquisition | Worker chooses whether to pay attention / gather information | Search effort, claiming, application quality, contract understanding | Response depends on monitoring, stakes, or information costs | Monitored attention designs; click/reading data; costly-information models |
| Dynamic learning | Information environment is processed gradually over repeated exposure | Earnings responses over time, claiming, saving, training navigation | Wedges shrink or evolve with experience and exposure | Panel/event-study designs; repeated interventions; dynamic structural models |

:::
