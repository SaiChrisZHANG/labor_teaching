## Identification diagnostics and common failures

| Problem | Diagnostic question | Typical empirical symptom | Practical response |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Setting–method disconnect | Does the chapter name the actual estimator, not just the setting? | “field experiment” is mentioned, but no regression or estimand is specified | require an explicit estimand + method sentence |
| Beliefs vs realized opportunities | Are biased beliefs being confused with poor labor-market conditions? | strong correlation between beliefs and outcomes with little validation | use repeated beliefs, validation, and controls for realized opportunities |
| Treatment vs measurement | Did the intervention change knowledge, salience, hassle, or motivation? | the treatment is described vaguely as “information” | decompose the information object and compare alternative mechanisms |
| Selection vs behavioral response | Are contract or job-choice effects partly compositional? | large treatment effects with endogenous take-up or sorting | separate treatment arms from contract-choice margins; consider selection models |
| Bunching over-interpretation | Does local bunching identify preferences or knowledge? | elasticity claims with little discussion of salience/learning | pair bunching evidence with knowledge data or information variation |
| Structural overreach | Is the chapter using structural language without explaining why? | parameter recovery appears disconnected from the identifying data moments | state clearly which moments identify which parameters and what assumptions matter |
| Welfare over-claiming | Does the design identify welfare or only behavior? | strong policy conclusions from one reduced-form response | separate behavior from welfare, learning, and equilibrium spillovers |
