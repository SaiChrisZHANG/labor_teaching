# Micro norms, peer pressure, and firm culture map

| Level of norm | Mechanism | Observable outcome | Representative design | Why it matters for labor |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Worker-level identity | Self-image, role congruence, occupational fit | Job choice, effort, willingness to enter or stay in jobs | theory + survey/admin links | Connects labor allocation to identity utility |
| Team-level norm | Peer monitoring, informal sanctions, comparison pressure | Shift productivity, effort, attendance, cooperation | within-team peer-exposure designs | Shows how local social pressure shapes workplace behavior |
| Compensation norm | Pay compression or disparity relative to peers | Morale, quits, output, pay satisfaction | wage-dispersion or info-provision experiments | Makes fairness a labor-market force |
| Firm culture | Shared values communicated to applicants and employees | Applicant composition, hiring, retention, match quality | job-postings text + inflow/outflow evidence | Makes culture measurable in labor markets |
| Manager norm | Supervisors transmit local expectations and promotion standards | Promotion, pay gaps, local culture persistence | manager assignment / rotation designs | Shows how norms move through organizations |
| Market-facing norm | Workers sort across firms based on perceived culture and fit | Worker–firm matching and reallocation | worker sorting / application response designs | Links micro culture to equilibrium allocation |
