# Incentives, monitoring, and supervision map

| Behavioral object | Contract / governance margin | Main labor outcome | Canonical paper(s) | Teaching use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Reciprocity / gift exchange | Employer generosity, unexpected gifts, presentation of pay | Extra work, effort, productivity | [@kubeMarechalPuppe2012]; [@dellaVignaListMalmendierRao2022] | Separate kindness effects from standard incentive effects |
| Reference dependence / loss aversion | Prepaid bonus, clawback framing, target-based rewards | Target-hitting, gaming, multitasking, total performance | [@hossainList2012]; [@pierceReesJonesBlank2025] | Show how framing can both motivate and distort |
| Standard incentives benchmark | Piece rates, bonuses, pay for performance | Output / effort response | [@dellaVignaPope2018] | Benchmark motivational channels before richer workplace designs |
| Heterogeneous incentive responsiveness | Performance-pay contract choice and treatment | Output, earnings inequality, sorting | [@bandieraFischerPratYtsma2021] | Distinguish average treatment from heterogeneous response |
| Monitoring as a managerial technology | Observation, performance feedback, supervisor attention | Measured effort, compliance, productivity | [@kelley2024monitoring] | Show how monitoring can complement or substitute for stronger pay incentives |
| Subjective supervision / evaluation | Supervisor ratings, discretionary performance assessment | Influence activity, measured performance, promotion or pay consequences | [@deJanvrySadouletSuriWang2023]; [@macleod2003subjective] | Distinguish productive effort from supervisor-facing effort |
