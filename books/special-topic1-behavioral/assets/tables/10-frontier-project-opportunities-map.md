## Week 10 table — frontier project opportunities map

| Frontier theme | Why it is open | Promising data / design direction | Example project question |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Dynamic learning of labor-market policies | Workers learn schedules, eligibility rules, and claiming procedures slowly and unevenly | Linked admin panels, repeated information interventions, distributed-lag/event-study designs | How quickly do displaced workers learn training subsidies, and who remains misinformed? |
| Belief formation in rapidly changing labor markets | Outside options and job-finding beliefs may adjust slowly to shocks and technology | Repeated belief elicitation + admin outcomes + vacancy data | How do workers update wage expectations after AI-driven task change? |
| Algorithmic interfaces in search and work | Platforms and digital tools may shape attention, application behavior, and productivity | Platform logs, randomized ranking/notification designs, survey-linkage data | Do recommendation systems amplify or attenuate biased search behavior? |
| Behavioral heterogeneity and targeting | Same intervention may help some workers and hurt others depending on bias type | Heterogeneity analysis, prediction/targeting exercises, adaptive policy trials | Which workers benefit most from simplified claiming or commitment options? |
| Firm responses to worker biases | Employers may exploit, insure, or sort on behavioral traits | Menu designs, default changes, benefit architecture, hiring interfaces | When do firms offer commitment or default products because workers demand them versus because they are profitable? |
| Welfare under endogenous learning | Short-run mistakes may shrink with experience, complicating paternalism | Repeated-choice panels, learning models, long-run follow-up | When do short-run nudges improve welfare once workers would have learned anyway? |
| Survey–admin–platform integration | Measuring beliefs, norms, and choice together is still rare | Matched survey-admin data, platform experiments, linked HR systems | Can linked belief and HR data identify when supervisor feedback changes perceived outside options? |
