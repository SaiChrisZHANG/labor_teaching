:::{table} Methods and welfare map for Behavioral Labor Week 1
:label: tbl:methods-welfare-week1

| Design / method | What it can identify | Typical labor use | Main welfare caution |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Field experiment | Causal treatment effect on a specific behavioral margin | Incentives, information, search assistance, workplace interventions | Treatment effects do not automatically reveal deep parameters |
| Information / salience intervention | Role of knowledge, attention, or complexity | Job search, take-up, application behavior, worker policy | Improved decisions may reflect both information and motivation |
| Observational design with behavioral measures | Correlation between beliefs/preferences and labor outcomes | Search behavior, training, retirement, labor supply | Selection and measurement error may remain central |
| Structural behavioral estimation | Parameters of a specific behavioral model | Present bias, search behavior, dynamic choice, policy counterfactuals | Welfare and counterfactuals depend on strong modeling choices |
| Reduced-form policy evaluation | Average response to a policy feature | Nudges, simplification, default rules, incentives | Need a clear normative benchmark before claiming welfare gains |

:::
