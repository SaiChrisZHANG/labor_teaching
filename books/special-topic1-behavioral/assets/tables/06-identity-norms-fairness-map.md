# Identity, norms, and fairness map

| Behavioral object | Core economic idea | Main labor margin | Canonical anchor(s) | Teaching use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Identity at work | Workers care about who they are and what kind of job/person they are expected to be | Job choice, effort, promotion aspirations, role fit | [@akerlofKranton2005] | Introduce identity as a labor-economic object, not just a sociological label |
| Fairness / relative pay | Utility depends on relative pay and treatment, not only own wage | Morale, effort, attendance, quits, pay satisfaction | [@brezaKaurShamdasani2018]; [@cullenPerezTruglia2018] | Distinguish absolute pay effects from social-comparison effects |
| Peer pressure / coworker norms | Coworkers discipline or encourage effort when behavior is visible | Productivity, effort, compliance, team behavior | [@masMoretti2009] | Separate peer learning from peer pressure |
| Firm culture | Firms differ in communicated and perceived values and practices | Sorting, recruitment, retention, match quality | [@huangPacelliShiZou2024] | Show culture as a labor-market object |
| Manager-driven norm transmission | Managers shape local workplace norms and opportunities | Promotions, pay gaps, team culture, within-firm inequality | [@minniNguyenSarsonsSrebot2026] | Surface the frontier on micro culture and transmission |
| Identity in broader labor allocation | Norms can shape labor supply and earnings choices outside the firm too | Labor supply, household allocation, earnings arrangements | [@bertrandKamenicaPan2015] | Give one brief example without turning the week into a gender or institutions lecture |
