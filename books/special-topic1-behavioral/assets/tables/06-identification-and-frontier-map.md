# Identification and frontier map

| Question | Behavioral object | Margin observed | Identification challenge | Frontier direction |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Are workers responding to peer pressure or peer learning? | Team norm / social sanction vs information | Productivity, effort, attendance | Visibility and reflection problems | Remote teams, digital monitoring, algorithmic coworker exposure |
| Do workers care about fairness or about information on career prospects? | Relative pay concern vs career belief update | Job satisfaction, quits, effort | Pay information changes several beliefs at once | Pay transparency with rich admin data and dynamic responses |
| Does culture attract workers or change workers after entry? | Firm culture as sorting vs treatment | Applicants, hires, quits, within-firm outcomes | Separating inflow composition from within-firm change | Job-posting text, reviews, and longer-run worker trajectories |
| Do managers transmit norms or simply differ in skill? | Manager-driven cultural transmission | Promotion, team composition, pay gaps | Assignment and quality confounding | Manager rotation, multinational exposures, spillovers across teams |
| Are identity-based effects local, workplace-based, or broader social norms? | Identity / reference-group utility | Labor supply, effort, occupation/job choice | Mapping the relevant reference group | Integrating micro workplace norms with broader norm environments |
| How do micro norms scale to labor-market allocation? | Sorting, retention, and local cultural fit | Worker–firm matching, inequality, mobility | Equilibrium selection and partial-equilibrium evidence | Linking culture measures to reallocation and inequality dynamics |
