## Week 10 table — research design template

| Step | Question to ask | Typical output | Common failure mode |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Puzzle / fact | What labor-market pattern is not well explained by the standard model? | One-sentence motivating fact | Pattern is interesting but not clearly labor-relevant |
| Behavioral wedge | What nonstandard preference, belief, attention, norm, or learning mechanism might matter? | Mechanism statement | Treating a generic friction as if it were already identified |
| Setting / institution | In what labor-market environment does the wedge operate? | Concrete context: search, take-up, workplace contract, saving, training, sorting | Setting too vague or not institutionally disciplined |
| Outcome / unit | What exactly is observed, and at what unit? | Worker, job, shift, claim, application, spell, firm, market | Outcome does not map cleanly to the mechanism |
| Design | What variation identifies the effect? | Experiment, quasi-experiment, panel learning design, bunching, structural design | Variation shifts many objects at once |
| Method | What econometric tool best matches the setting? | Hazard, FE panel, ANCOVA, bunching, event study, structural SMM/MLE | Method chosen by habit rather than by estimand |
| Welfare / interpretation | What makes the result normatively or policy relevant? | Welfare benchmark or explicit interpretation limits | Implicit welfare claims without a framework |
| Contribution | Why is this not just a rerun of an existing paper? | New setting, new margin, new data, new equilibrium implication, new welfare object | Incrementalism without a contribution margin |
