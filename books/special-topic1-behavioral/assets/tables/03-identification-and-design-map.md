:::{table} Identification and design map for behavioral job search
:label: tbl:behavioral-job-search-designs

| Design / method | What it can identify well | What it struggles with | Good use in this week |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Elicited beliefs panel | subjective job-finding beliefs and revisions | separating beliefs from all unobserved state variables | optimism, duration dependence, updating |
| Information-provision field experiment | causal effect of advice/information on search outcomes | why the information matters psychologically | search strategies and employment prospects |
| Skill-assessment / certification experiment | effect of self-knowledge and credible signaling | general equilibrium hiring effects | two-sided information and match quality |
| Wage-posting field experiment | response to wage signals and perceived competition | long-run equilibrium search adjustment | vacancy choice and application behavior |
| Application-cost intervention | importance of small search frictions | disentangling liquidity from salience | application volume and talent selection |
| Structural search model with beliefs | disciplined counterfactuals and welfare | model dependence | mapping subjective beliefs into behavior and policy |

:::
