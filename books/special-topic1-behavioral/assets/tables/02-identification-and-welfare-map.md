:::{table} Identification and welfare issues for worker-side nonstandard preferences
:label: tbl:behavioral-pref-id-welfare
| Object | Main empirical challenge | Key confounders to rule out | Primary worker-side outcomes | Welfare complication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Present bias | separating self-control problems from effort costs or liquidity constraints | cash-flow constraints, learning, standard fatigue | effort, labor supply timing, task completion, training completion | ex ante self vs ex post self may disagree |
| Commitment demand | showing true demand for discipline rather than correlated preferences or firm screening | unobserved productivity, employer assignment, monitoring technology | contract choice, saving-plan demand, training structure demand | commitment may raise productivity while restricting choice |
| Savings under self-control problems | separating procrastination from transaction costs or information frictions | financial literacy, administrative hassle, employer contribution design | retirement saving, contribution rates, savings participation | observed saving may not reveal long-run welfare |
| Reference dependence | distinguishing target/reference-point behavior from standard nonlinear incentives | dynamic learning, fatigue, income effects, composition | labor supply, effort, performance | evaluated utility depends on reference point choice and adaptation |
| Social preferences | separating reciprocity from monitoring or repeated-game incentives | supervision, career concerns, selection, social pressure | workplace effort, cooperation, morale | welfare depends on whether higher effort is intrinsically valued or induced by pressure |
:::
