:::{table} Behavioral job search wedges: the main objects of the week
:label: tbl:behavioral-job-search-wedges

| Behavioral object | Search margin affected | Typical empirical handle | Main risk of misinterpretation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Biased job-finding beliefs | search effort, reservation wage, duration | elicited beliefs, longitudinal surveys | confounding beliefs with true heterogeneity |
| Weak updating during unemployment | duration dependence, effort decay, reservation behavior | repeated belief elicitation over spells | mistaking true opportunity decline for behavioral bias |
| Limited self-knowledge about skills | targeting, applications, match quality | skill assessments, certification experiments | confusing self-knowledge with pure employer screening |
| Perceived competition from posted wages | vacancy choice, applications, self-selection | randomized wage announcements | interpreting all wage effects as price effects only |
| Salient or fixed application costs | application volume, who applies, selection | fee/subsidy interventions, friction manipulations | conflating attention with liquidity or transport costs |
| Advice / information provision | strategy choice, job targeting, employment outcomes | brochures, personalized suggestions, search coaching | mistaking motivation or monitoring effects for information effects |

:::
